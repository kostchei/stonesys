12. Patrons

This chapter applies in campaigns whose playbooks carry patron tracks. If your sheet has no Bond/Debt/Heat rows, skip it.

A patron is a powerful backer: a noble house, thieves' guild, merchant cartel, temple, hidden master, wizard, crime lord, military order, ancient spirit, or distant empire. A patron makes power transactional — they give access, protection, favors, secrets, magic, work, and trouble.

Your relationship with a patron lives on three tracks on your sheet's embedment rail.

Bond (0–3) — how much they trust you

  • 0 — Known contact. The patron recognizes you.
  • 1 — Useful asset. They'll spend minor resources on you.
  • 2 — Trusted agent. Serious aid, sensitive work.
  • 3 — Marked favorite. Your success is part of their power.

Raise Bond by completing a patron's demand, protecting their interests at real cost, bringing them rare treasure, or expanding their influence. Lower Bond by refusing a demand, embarrassing them, aiding a rival, breaking their taboo, or failing in a way that damages their agenda.

Debt (0–6) — what they can call in

Add 1 Debt when you: ask for help without paying now · invoke the patron while in danger · use their name to gain access · fail a patron move · keep a gift after refusing its price.

Clear Debt when you: complete a demand · pay a costly tithe, secret, service, hostage, oath, or treasure · accept lasting fallout on the patron's behalf.

At Debt 6 the patron makes a major demand immediately. Once you accept or resolve it, Debt resets to 3.

Heat (0–4) — who's watching

Attention from rivals, authorities, enemies, and suspicious locals.

0 quiet · 1 rumors · 2 watched · 3 targeted · 4 open retaliation.

Heat rises when patron business becomes public, when you act violently or dramatically, or when rivals are provoked. It falls through time, bribes, diplomacy, scapegoats, or decisive victories.

The move: Invoke Patron

When you ask a patron for immediate aid, say what you want and what leverage you have. Build the pool:

  • Positive: green dice equal to your Bond. +1 blue if you have a relevant asset or connection. You may always add 1 Debt to upgrade one green to yellow.
  • Negative: difficulty by the request — modest average (2), dangerous hard (3), anything that could expose or weaken the patron daunting (4). +1 black die per point of your current Heat.

Success with ▲ — choose two:

  • The aid arrives quickly.
  • The aid is exactly what you asked for.
  • No new Debt.
  • Heat does not rise.
  • The patron reveals useful extra information.

Clean success — choose one from the list above, and one cost from the list below.

Success with ▼ — choose one from the list above; the GM also chooses one:

  • Add 1 Debt.
  • Add 1 Heat.
  • The aid is partial, delayed, or compromised.
  • The patron attaches a demand.
  • A rival learns about the request.

Failure: the patron may still answer if it's interesting, but things turn: add 1 Debt and 1 Heat, or the patron is offended.

What patrons give

Shelter · legal cover · forged papers · rumors · safe passage · a specialist hireling · a squad of rough allies · a minor magic item · a curse lifted for a while · a door opened in high society · a rival distracted · a secret revealed · support for the steading.

What patrons ask

When Debt comes due, expect work: recover a stolen object, silence a witness, protect a shipment, spy on a rival, escort a dangerous guest, break a taboo to prove loyalty, deliver tribute, endorse the patron publicly, sabotage a rival's works, hide someone hunted, enter a ruin for something unnamed, or accept a visible mark of service. Saying no is allowed — and costs Bond.
