13. Gods

This chapter applies in campaigns whose playbooks carry divine tracks. If your sheet has no Devotion/Favor/Wrath rows, skip it.

A god is a power of worship, fear, place, and portfolio: a true deity, an ancestor host, a saint, a demon-god, a river's spirit, a dead empire's idol, a city cult. Gods are more public than patrons and more dangerous to treat casually. A god's portfolio — storms and sea, hearth and oaths, secrets and thieves, war, mercy, fate — defines what it can easily touch.

Everyday religion

Most people honor many gods: a farmer prays to rain, sun, hearth, roads, birth, and death in the same month. Exclusive devotion is for priests, zealots, chosen heroes, and the desperate. This matters at the table:

  • Anyone can make offerings and receive omens.
  • Breaking a local god's taboo can hurt you even if you follow another.
  • Rival gods can both hold legitimate claims on you.

Devotion (0–3) — your standing

  • 0 — occasional worshipper · 1 — sworn faithful · 2 — favored servant · 3 — chosen instrument.

Gain it by upholding a tenet at cost, completing a holy task, making a meaningful offering, protecting the god's people, restoring a shrine, or defeating a rival power. Lose it by breaking a taboo, profaning a holy place, ignoring a direct omen, aiding a hated rival, or using divine gifts selfishly.

Favor (0–3) — spendable grace

Favor is a hold (chapter 9) granted by the god. You can carry at most 3 from one god.

Gain 1 Favor when you complete a rite, sacrifice treasure, fulfill a vow, or roll success with ▲ on a prayer.

Spend 1 Favor to:

  • Add 1 blue die to a check that clearly serves the god's portfolio.
  • Ask the GM for an omen.
  • Turn success-with-▼ on a holy act into success-with-▲.
  • Protect an ally from a minor curse, fear, or spiritual harm.
  • Declare a small religious contact, shrine, or sign exists nearby.

The move: Pray for Aid

When you pray for divine aid under pressure, name the god, state the request, and offer something meaningful. Build the pool:

  • Positive: green dice equal to your Devotion. +1 blue for a relevant offering or holy relic. You may spend 1 Favor to upgrade one green to yellow.
  • Negative: difficulty set by the GM — usually average (2) or hard (3).

Success with ▲ — the god intervenes clearly. Choose one:

  • Healing or relief from a condition.
  • Protection from a specific danger.
  • Revelation of a hidden truth.
  • A weapon, tool, or spell empowered for one scene.
  • A hostile spirit, undead, curse, or demon forced back.
  • A path opened where none seemed available.

Success with ▼ — the aid comes with a condition. Choose one:

  • Mark 1 Favor owed as a vow.
  • The effect is brief or incomplete.
  • The god demands visible obedience, now.
  • A rival power notices.
  • The answer is symbolic and must be interpreted.

Failure: the god is silent, offended, or terrifyingly present. Things turn — and Wrath may rise.

Wrath (0–4) — divine displeasure

  • 0 none · 1 bad omens · 2 blessings fail or turn unreliable · 3 the god sends agents, dreams, curses, or monsters · 4 you are excommunicated, cursed, hunted, or spiritually marked.

Reduce Wrath through confession, restitution, pilgrimage, sacrifice, completing a hard command, or making peace with the offended power. Wrath never fades on its own.

Offerings

An offering should cost something.

OfferingUsual effect
Coin, gems, or treasureGain Favor, if the amount matters to you
Food, wine, incense, candlesSeek an omen or minor blessing
Blood, pain, fasting, ordealAsk protection, cleansing, or power
A vow, oath, or public serviceGain Devotion when fulfilled
A relic, enemy trophy, or reclaimed shrineReduce Wrath or raise Devotion
