20. The Sign of the Black Cat

The premise

In the poorest, oldest quarter of a great, fog-choked, god-ridden city — where the fog never fully lifts and the watch never fully enters — a band of ne'er-do-wells works out of a rented cellar under a failing wine-shop. The faded sign above gives the band its name. The room below is smoke-stained, the chairs are mismatched, the door is good and strong, and there is a bolt-hole behind the wine-casks and a black cat no one admits to feeding.

The city runs on guilds, and the guilds mean it: thieving without a license is a hanging matter twice over — once by the law, once by the Thieves' Guild. A hundred small gods jostle for worshippers along a single crowded street, and some of them answer. The band's ambitions are simple: stay fed, stay unhanged, and pull off the one score that buys a better life — or at least a better cellar.

Your home

The steading is the cellar-den (Scale −1, Safety +1, Prosperity 0, Influence +1, Supply 0), with two facilities: the Cellar-Den itself, which makes a person or a thing disappear for a while but is one betrayed door away from a trap with no way out; and the Fence's Corner, which moves hot goods quietly — though every sale leaves a thread the Thieves' Guild can pull.

Beneath the floor lies the den's secret and strange resource: the Underway, a forgotten branch of the city's ancient drains and smugglers' tunnels — a way under half the quarter, known to almost no one, mapped only in one scavenger's head. What uses the Underway does not only run one direction.

People your community ties point at: Old Sabra, who keeps the den, pays the rent, and remembers every favor owed in the quarter; Feathers, the guild's soft-spoken collector, friendly right up until he isn't; Brother Gnarl, a gutter-priest of a small, spiteful god, right about omens just often enough to be frightening; and Wren, a lookout too young for this life, whom the whole band silently protects.

Embedment style

Power-structure, patron-heavy. The guilds and brotherhoods are patrons (chapter 12); the street's small gods take worship and give trouble (chapter 13); the Dabbler in the Black Arts carries a Corruption personal track (chapter 14); and the den itself is a light steading (chapter 15) — expect Heat to matter more here than anywhere else in this book.

The Guild Beggar-Spy

No one sees a beggar. That is your whole power. You are a sworn member of the Beggars' Guild — the city's true eyes and ears — sitting invisible on every corner, running a hundred ragged children and cripples who between them see everything that happens in Lankhmar. You trade in the one thing worth more than stolen gold here: knowing.

Duty: The Guild made you invisible, and invisibility is a leash. Every secret you gather, the Beggar King is owed a share of. Sell one behind the Guild's back at your peril.

Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

Eyes on Every Corner · Signature

When you put your beggars' network to work gathering word on a person, place, or plan.

Roll +CHA · default difficulty average.

  • Success: Word comes back: you learn something true and useful about your subject.
  • Failure: The word is thin, late, or bought — and someone knows you were asking.
  • ▲ spend: Hold 1 Whisper (spend: a beggar saw exactly the thing you need / an urchin is already in place to watch or follow / a coded warning reaches you the moment danger moves / a secret on someone present, held in reserve). / You learn who else has been paying for word on the same subject.
  • ▼ costs: One of your people is seen, hurt, or turned; the network frays. / Add 1 Heat: your asking reaches an ear that reaches back.
  • ◆ Triumph: The whole city's gossip pours in; you know your subject's business better than they do.
  • ✶ Despair: Your network has been fed a lie, or infiltrated, and you're acting on poison.

Hold — Whispers. Spend 1 to: A beggar saw exactly the thing you now need to know. / An urchin is already in place to watch, follow, or carry a message. / A coded warning reaches you the instant danger moves. / You hold a secret on someone present, kept for the right moment.

The Beggar King's Due · Fixed

When you call on the Beggars' Guild for shelter, a swarm of witnesses, a hidden road, or the city's gossip (Invoke Patron — chapter 12):

The Guild provides — no one commands so many invisible eyes — and adds 1 Debt. The Beggar King is owed a share of every secret you gather; hoard from him, or sell what he has not been paid for, and you will find how quickly the invisible can become the hunted.

Unseen in Plain Sight · Choice

When you hide as what no one looks at — a beggar, a servant, a nothing — in plain view.

Roll +CHA · default difficulty average.

  • Success: You are simply not noticed; you can watch, wait, or slip past as furniture.
  • Failure: Someone looks twice, and the wrong someone.
  • ▲ spend: Hide another in the crowd with you. / You overhear something worth the wait.
  • ▼ costs: Your guise slips a little; a detail is remembered. / You're in place, but boxed in.
  • ◆ Triumph: You could stand in the room while they discuss killing you, and they'd never know.
  • ✶ Despair: The one who sees through you is exactly the one who must not.

A Hundred Faces · Choice

When you take on a disguise or a false life to get somewhere you don't belong.

Roll +CHA · default difficulty average.

  • Success: They take you for who you pretend to be, for now.
  • Failure: The disguise won't hold here, or the wrong person knows the real you.
  • ▲ spend: They buy it until you give them a hard reason not to. / You learn what only the disguised role would be told.
  • ▼ costs: The act costs you a prop, a coin, or a slip. / Someone files your false face away for later.
  • ◆ Triumph: You become someone so convincingly you're welcomed and trusted.
  • ✶ Despair: You've assumed the identity of someone with dangerous enemies, and just met one.

Read the Street · Choice

When you take the temper of a crowd, a quarter, or a room from its small signs.

Roll +WIS · default difficulty average.

  • Success: Ask the GM one: who's afraid here? who's lying? where's the trouble about to start? Acting on it grants a boost die.
  • Failure: You misread the mood, and it turns under you.
  • ▲ spend: Ask another. / You spot the informant, the tail, or the trap in the crowd.
  • ▼ costs: Reading them, you were read in turn.
  • ◆ Triumph: You feel the whole street's mood like a hand on a pulse.
  • ✶ Despair: The calm you read was the calm before it fell on you.

Trade in Secrets · Choice

When you barter a secret you hold for coin, a favor, or another secret:

Take a boost die; what you know is worth more here than gold. But every secret sold is a secret loosed, and the Beggar King expects his cut of the deal. The GM says who else learns you were dealing.
