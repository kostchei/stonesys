6. The Character Sheet

Your sheet — printed A3 or the web version — has five zones. Read it top to bottom, left to right.

Header

The playbook's name, your character's name, your Look (a few chosen descriptors), and the duty line in bold. The duty is not flavor text: it is the standing pressure your archetype lives under — the choice or burden that defines the role — and the GM will lean on it.

Left rail — stats and vitals

The six stats, each rated 1–5:

StatCovers
STRstrength: force, heavy lifting, raw muscle
DEXdexterity: speed, stealth, aim, balance
CONconstitution: endurance, toughness, resisting harm and hardship
INTintelligence: lore, logic, deciphering, wards and workings
WISwisdom: perception, instinct, sensing spirits and moods
CHAcharisma: persuasion, leadership, presence

The number is your dice. WIS 3 means three green dice whenever a move says "roll +WIS." The sheet prints the dice count under each stat.

Below the stats:

  • HP — hit points, current and maximum. Your playbook sets the maximum (usually a base plus CON). When you take harm, HP goes down (chapter 10).
  • Damage die — what you roll when the fiction says you hurt something (d6, d8, or d10, set by your playbook).
  • Load — how much you can carry without slowing down, set by your playbook (usually a base plus STR).
  • XP — experience marks (chapter 11).
  • Conditions — written next to HP as you acquire them (chapter 9).

Center — moves

Everything your character can do beyond the obvious, in three kinds:

  • Signature (bordered): always on. The move that is your archetype, and usually the one that earns its named hold currency.
  • Fixed: always on. Often plain text — a standing truth about your character rather than a roll.
  • Choice (with checkboxes): you have the ones that are checked; take more through advancement.

Chapter 8 explains how to use them.

Right rail — embedment

Who your character is entangled with. Depending on the campaign this shows:

  • Power-structure tracks — rows of pips for Bond/Debt/Heat with a patron, Devotion/Favor/Wrath with a god, Standing with your steading, or a personal track such as Defilement or Corruption (Parts III–IV). Each row's note states its threshold rule.
  • Bonds — fill-in-the-blank statements naming other player characters: "____ fought beside me when it counted." Bonds say who you are to each other, and resolving one earns XP.
  • Community tie — your personal hook into the steading: a person, a place, or a debt that makes home yours.

Some campaigns (the Hollow-Oak Holt) have no tracks at all — the bonds are the embedment.

Footer — advancement and gear

The advancement checklist (chapter 11) and your gear picks — the equipment choices your playbook offered at creation, plus whatever you've gained since.
