18. The Free Legion

The premise

Under a dying sun, on a world of ash, salt, and bone, an army of escaped gladiator-slaves is on the move. The city's heralds call them "the broken chain." They call themselves the Free Legion — a straggle of mismatched arms, sun-bleached tents, and yoked pack-beasts, ringed each dusk by a thorn-and-spear stockade, never sleeping twice in the same dry wash. Your characters broke the pens or joined the column after. Now comes the hard part: surviving the pursuit, holding the freed together, and finding something worth calling home.

This is a world where metal is treasure, water is politics, and magic has a price the land itself pays. Wizard-tyrants rule the cities through templar bureaucracies; the faiths that answer prayers belong to the raw elements; and every fire lit by sorcery leaves the ground a little more dead — unless the caster is one of the rare few who take gently.

Your home

The steading is the Legion itself: a mobile war-camp (Scale +1, Safety −1, Prosperity 0, Influence +1, Supply 0), with two facilities on the move — the Forge-Wagon, which turns broken loot into usable weapons overnight but goes cold without smith or fuel, and the Field-Infirmary, which keeps the badly hurt alive through one more march but collapses fast when water runs short.

The Legion guards one strange resource: the Iron Coffer, a locked chest of true sword-steel in a land of bone and obsidian — a few dozen blades' worth, no more. It arms the Legion's best, and it draws every raider, merchant house, and templar who hears the rumor.

People your community ties point at: Karth One-Hand, the war-leader who struck the first blow in the pens, tired and harder than he lets on; Senna, the quartermaster who actually keeps the Legion alive and resents that no one thanks her; Little Vurt, a freed child the whole camp has silently agreed to protect; and the Veiled One, a mage the Legion needs and fears — because no one is sure whether the magic is the gentle kind or the kind that kills the ground.

What the Legion needs cannot be bought: legitimacy and water security — a place and a claim that make it a people the world must reckon with, not a mob to be hunted down.

Embedment style

Power-structure, patron-heavy. Most playbooks here answer to someone: a hidden alliance of gentle mages, a merchant house, a sorcerer-king's bureaucracy, a performers' guild, an elf raiding tribe (chapter 12). The Elemental Cleric serves an element as a god (chapter 13). The Defiler and the Preserver carry personal tracks — Defilement and Scars — that never heal (chapter 14). The Gladiator and the Legion's own are embedded in the steading itself.

Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

Gentle Working · Signature

When you shape arcane power while sparing the living land around you.

Roll +INT · default difficulty average.

  • Success: The working does what you intend, and the ground still lives.
  • Failure: The power will not come gently; to get anything you must reach for the defiler's way, or let it fail.
  • ▲ spend: Hold 1 Life (spend: extend or bend the effect / shield a bystander / sense nearby magic / leave no scar at all). / You learn something true about the magic in this place.
  • ▼ costs: The effort costs you: take a setback die until you rest. / Your working flares where the wrong eyes can see it.
  • ◆ Triumph: The land itself leans into your working; the effect is greater and clean.
  • ✶ Despair: The power slips toward defiling. Mark a Scar, or the GM defiles the ground in your place.

Hold — Life. Spend 1 to: Extend, curve, or split the effect to reach what you first could not. / Throw a shield of living force over an ally in reach. / Sense the shape and source of magic around you. / Leave no defiled scar, even on a hard working.

Veiled · Fixed

Because you belong to a hidden alliance of preservers:

You can find safe houses, fellow mages, and quiet aid where the alliance reaches (Invoke Patron — chapter 12). But every use risks exposing them; careless invocations raise Heat for the whole alliance, not just you.

Read the Weave · Choice

When you study a magic, a ward, or a worked thing.

Roll +INT · default difficulty average.

  • Success: Ask the GM one: who made this? what does it cost to use? how is it bound, and how undone?
  • Failure: You misread it, and the GM tells you how that misreading bites.
  • ▲ spend: Ask another question. / You can borrow or copy a small part of the working.
  • ▼ costs: Studying it leaves a mark the maker can feel. / What you learn unsettles you: setback on your next working here.
  • ◆ Triumph: You grasp the working whole, well enough to break or rebuild it.
  • ✶ Despair: The thing you studied was watching back.

Coax the Waste · Choice

When you search a dead land for the life still hiding in it.

Roll +WIS · default difficulty average.

  • Success: You find water, forage, shade, or a living thing the waste was keeping secret.
  • Failure: There is nothing here, and looking has cost you time and strength.
  • ▲ spend: Enough for the whole Legion, not just you. / You spot the danger that guards it before it spots you.
  • ▼ costs: What you found is claimed, fouled, or watched. / The search drew something hungry.
  • ◆ Triumph: You find a hidden seep or grove worth coming back to.
  • ✶ Despair: The only life here is something that should have stayed buried.

Ward · Choice

When you raise a protective working over a person, a camp, or a threshold:

Name the harm it turns aside (a kind of magic, fire, prying eyes, the heat). It holds until it is overwhelmed or you let it fall. While it stands, those it guards take a boost die against that harm.

Quiet the Power · Choice

When backlash, a curse, or the pull toward defiling tries to take you.

Roll +CON · default difficulty hard.

  • Success: You hold. The power does not have you, and the land is spared.
  • Failure: It takes its due: defile the ground, mark a Scar, or fall.
  • ▲ spend: You shield another caught in the same backlash.
  • ▼ costs: You hold, but it leaves you shaking: setback until you rest.
  • ◆ Triumph: You turn the backlash into a clean, controlled release.
  • ✶ Despair: Something in you breaks a little toward the defiler's hunger.

The Elven Dune-Raider

You run the open waste on legs that never tire, raiding and trading and vanishing before the dust settles. Your tribe's oaths are the whole of your honor; an outsider's word is wind. The Free Legion is still learning which of those you offer them.

Duty: Keep faith with the tribe and you are never alone in the waste. Break an elf-oath and you are worse than dead to them, and to yourself.

Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

Run the Waste · Signature

When you cross the open desert at the elven pace, or run down a quarry, or vanish into the dunes.

Roll +CON · default difficulty average.

  • Success: You get there, or get away, faster than anything on foot can follow.
  • Failure: The waste beats you this time: too far, too dry, or too watched.
  • ▲ spend: Hold 1 Stride (spend: bring others along at your pace / arrive unseen / find water or a landmark on the way / choose the ground where you stop). / You read the land ahead — where the danger and the water lie.
  • ▼ costs: The run costs you: thirst and strain, a setback die until you rest. / You are spotted, or you outrun your own supply.
  • ◆ Triumph: You cover impossible ground and arrive fresh.
  • ✶ Despair: The waste turns on you — a storm, a predator, a dead end with no way back.

Hold — Stride. Spend 1 to: Bring companions along at your pace, this once. / Arrive unseen and unexpected. / Find water, forage, or a landmark on the way. / Choose the ground where the run ends.

Tribe Oaths · Fixed

When you call on your raiding tribe for shelter, arms, or riders (Invoke Patron — chapter 12):

They answer if you have kept faith, and add 1 Debt. But the tribe holds outsiders — the Legion among them — in open contempt, and an elf-oath broken makes you nothing to them. Weigh whose oaths you keep.

Raider's Bow · Choice

When you strike from the saddle or the ridge and are gone before they close.

Roll +DEX · default difficulty average.

  • Success: Your shot lands, and you are already moving.
  • Failure: You are drawn into the fight you meant to avoid.
  • ▲ spend: Drop a key foe. / Keep the range: they cannot reach you.
  • ▼ costs: You loose your last good arrows. / They mark your line of retreat.
  • ◆ Triumph: You break them without ever letting them near.
  • ✶ Despair: They have a way to catch you, and you just showed them where you are.

Desert Craft · Choice

When you read the waste for water, weather, trail, or threat.

Roll +WIS · default difficulty average.

  • Success: The desert tells you what you need: where the water is, what the sky will do, who passed and when.
  • Failure: The waste keeps its secrets, and the not-knowing will cost you.
  • ▲ spend: Enough foresight to guide the whole Legion true. / You spot the danger before it spots you.
  • ▼ costs: What you read is already changing. / Reading the trail, you left one of your own.
  • ◆ Triumph: You know the land as if you shaped it.
  • ✶ Despair: You misread the sky, and lead everyone into it.

Fleet and Unseen · Choice

When you move fast and hidden across open or broken ground.

Roll +DEX · default difficulty average.

  • Success: You go where you like, quick and unseen.
  • Failure: You are seen, or the ground defeats your speed.
  • ▲ spend: Take one other with you, just as hidden. / Leave no trail worth following.
  • ▼ costs: You are quick but not quiet, or quiet but not quick — pick your cost.
  • ◆ Triumph: You pass through them like wind through grass.
  • ✶ Despair: You run straight into what you were hiding from.

An Elf's Bargain · Choice

When you strike a sharp trade with an outsider:

Take a boost die to drive the price your way. But an elf's bargain is a famous thing: they will expect the letter of the deal and no more, and so will you. The GM plays every gap you leave.
