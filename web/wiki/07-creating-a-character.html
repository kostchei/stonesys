7. Creating a Character

Do this together, as a table, at the start of the campaign. It takes about half an hour.

1. Pick your campaign's playset

Each campaign chapter in Part V offers a steading and nine playbooks. The whole table plays in one playset; each player takes a different playbook.

2. Pick a playbook

Read the concepts aloud and claim what calls to you. The playbook gives you almost everything: default stats, HP and damage die, moves, gear options, bonds, and your community tie.

3. Set your stats

Every playbook distributes the array {4, 3, 3, 2, 2, 1} across STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA in a default arrangement that suits the archetype. Take the default, or swap any two values — up to two swaps. One rule binds you: your playbook's anchor stat (the one its signature move rolls) has a printed minimum, and no swap may take it below that.

4. Name yourself and choose your Look

Pick or invent a name from the playbook's list, and choose one option from each Look line (bearing, dress, eyes, and so on). Say what the others see.

5. Note your derived numbers

Copy from the playbook: maximum HP (base + CON), damage die, and Load (base + STR). Start at full HP, 0 XP.

6. Take your starting moves

You start with your signature move, every fixed move, and the playbook's stated number of choice moves (usually two — the sheet marks how many). Check them off.

7. Pick your gear

The playbook offers a short list of gear choices — "choose one of…" lines. Pick, and note anything with a special rule. Keep total weight within Load.

8. Write your bonds

The playbook gives fill-in-the-blank bond statements. Once everyone has introduced their character, fill in at least two with other characters' names. Read them aloud — bonds are promises about how your stories tangle.

9. Claim your community tie

Choose one option from the playbook's community-tie list. It names a person, place, or obligation in the steading that is yours. This is the GM's standing invitation to make home matter to you.

10. Set your embedment tracks

If your playbook carries power-structure tracks (Bond, Debt, Heat, Devotion, Favor, Wrath, Standing, or a personal track), set each pip to its printed starting value. The campaign chapters explain what each track means.

11. Introduce yourselves

Go around the table: name, Look, duty, and your community tie. The GM will ask questions. Answer them — every answer becomes true.

The one-page version of this checklist is Appendix A.
