21. Appendices

Appendix A — Character creation checklist

  1. Table picks one playset (Part V); each player claims a playbook.
  2. Stats: take the default array {4,3,3,2,2,1} or swap any two values (max two swaps; never below the anchor stat's minimum).
  3. Name and Look: one option per line.
  4. Note maximum HP, damage die, Load. Start at full HP, 0 XP.
  5. Moves: signature + all fixed + the stated number of choice moves.
  6. Gear: one pick per line, within Load.
  7. Bonds: fill in at least two with other characters' names.
  8. Community tie: choose one.
  9. Tracks: set pips to printed starting values.
  10. Introduce yourself: name, Look, duty, tie. Answer the GM's questions.

Building the pool: greens = stat (1–5) · trained: upgrade 1 green → yellow · risky (default): at least 1 yellow and 1 red, always · blues/blacks from the fiction, one per meaningful edge/hindrance.

The rails (nothing breaks them): max 5 dice per side (blues/blacks exempt) · max 2 yellow · max 2 red · overflow upgrades become blue/black.

Story Point: upgrade one die (green→yellow, or GM purple→red) or declare a small true detail. Spent token switches sides.

Appendix E — Harm reference

Taking harm: subtract armor from incoming damage, then subtract the remainder from HP. Armor defaults to 0 and does not stack unless gear says +1 armor. Armor is ignored by attacks that ignore armor or bypass physical defense.

Dealing harm: roll your playbook damage die. Do not add STR or DEX. Weapons decide reach, plausibility, and tags; only special gear adds damage.

Tag or ruleEffect

damage disadvantageRoll the damage die twice and keep the lower result, then add flat bonuses.

Recovery: Recover heals 4 + Prosperity HP for 1 use of supplies and cannot be used again until you take more damage. Make Camp heals half max HP, rounded up, or clears a condition. A bedroll adds +1d6 HP.

Death's Door: at 0 HP from a lethal source, roll the flat risky Death's Door check. A strong result returns you to 1 HP; a weak result leaves you alive but out of action; failure means death, revenant or ghost, or a bargain with the Things Below.

Appendix F — Glossary

  • Advantage (▲) — positive side-effect symbol; cancels threat one for one; spent on menus, holds, and edges.
  • Anchor stat — the stat a playbook's signature move rolls; swaps can't take it below its printed minimum.
  • Bond (track) — a patron's trust, 0–3; also Bonds, the fill-in statements tying characters together.
  • Boost die — blue d6, a momentary edge.
  • Challenge die — red d12, real peril; the only die with ✶.
  • Check — a roll of the dice pool.
  • Condition — lasting state naming two stats; +1 black on rolls with either until treated.
  • Debt — what a patron can call in, 0–6.
  • Despair (✶) — red-die symbol; a dramatic turn against you; counts one failure; never cancelled.
  • Devotion — standing with a god, 0–3.
  • Difficulty die — purple d8; the task rolling against you.
  • Duty — the standing pressure printed on your playbook's header.
  • Embedment — the sheet's right rail: tracks, bonds, community tie.
  • Facility — a working part of the steading, with a function and a vulnerability.
  • Favor — spendable divine hold, max 3 per god.
  • Fiction first — what's true in the story decides what the dice do, never the reverse.
  • Fortunes — the steading's confidence, 1–5, rolled on season moves.
  • GM — the player who runs the world.
  • Heat — unwanted attention, 0–4.
  • Hold — stored agency with a name (Strings, Momentum…), earned via ▲, spent 1:1.
  • HP — hit points; harm comes off them; 0 is death's door.
  • Load — carrying capacity.
  • Move — a rule with a fictional trigger; rollable or static.
  • Playbook — a ready-made archetype: stats, moves, gear, ties.
  • Pool — the dice you throw: positive side vs negative side.
  • Proficiency die — yellow d12, trained mastery; the only die with ◆.
  • Risky — carrying real stakes; forces at least 1 yellow and 1 red.
  • Season move — the steading's once-a-season roll on Fortunes.
  • Setback die — black d6, a momentary hindrance.
  • Standing — your personal credit with the steading, 0–3.
  • Steading — the party's shared home; a character with its own sheet.
  • Steading turn — a downtime order rolled on a rating.
  • Story Point — shared table currency; upgrade a die or declare a detail; spending flips it to the other side.
  • Surplus — the steading's stored plenty; its hold currency.
  • Threat (▼) — negative side-effect symbol; cancels advantage.
  • Track — a pip row on a sheet: standings and pressures, never dice.
  • Triumph (◆) — yellow-die symbol; a remarkable turn in your favor; counts one success; never cancelled.
  • Trouble — pressure on the steading, 0–4.
  • XP — experience: marked on failures and triggers; 5 buys an advancement.
