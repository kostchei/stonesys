# Steading: The Free Legion

Setting: Dark Sun playset. Original, unofficial adaptation.

A Bastion sheet (see `core.md`) for the shared home of the Dark Sun playset: an
escaped gladiator-slave army, still on the move, trying to become a people
instead of a mob of fugitives.

## Bastion Sheet

- **Name:** The Free Legion (the city's heralds call them "the broken chain").
- **Location:** a moving war-camp in the scrub and broken rock a few days out
  from a great walled city, never sleeping twice in the same dry wash.
- **Look:** a long straggle of mismatched arms, sun-bleached tents, and yoked
  pack-beasts, ringed each dusk by a thorn-and-spear stockade.
- **Founder / owner:** led, for now, by the gladiators who broke the pens. No
  one fully trusts that this will last.
- **Purpose:** survive the pursuit, hold the freed together, and find something
  worth calling home.
- **Scale:** `+1`  ·  **Safety:** `-1`  ·  **Prosperity:** `0`  ·  **Influence:** `+1`  ·  **Supply:** `0`

### Facilities

1. **The Forge-Wagon.** Function: repair and reforge arms from scavenged scrap.
   Rating used: Supply. Does especially well: turning broken loot into usable
   weapons overnight. Vulnerability: needs the smith and fuel; lose either and it
   goes cold.
2. **The Field-Infirmary.** Function: tend wounds, fever, and heat-sickness.
   Rating used: Supply. Does especially well: keeping the badly hurt alive
   through one more march. Vulnerability: collapses fast when water runs short.

### Trouble

`1` — grumbling over water rations and over where the Legion is even going.

### Strange resource

**The Iron Coffer.** A locked chest of true metal — sword-steel, scarce and
coveted in a land of bone and obsidian. It arms the Legion's best and draws every
raider, house, and templar who hears the rumor of it.

- Visible sign: a few fighters bear real steel; everyone notices.
- Scarcity: a few dozen blades' worth, no more.
- Side effect: its existence is a magnet for violence and betrayal.
- Who wants it: a merchant house, the city's templars, an elf raiding tribe.
- When it runs out: the Legion's edge — literal and reputational — is gone.

### People (community-tie hooks)

- **Karth One-Hand**, the war-leader who struck the first blow in the pens; tired
  and harder than he lets on.
- **Senna**, the quartermaster, who actually keeps the Legion alive and resents
  that no one thanks her for it.
- **Little Vurt**, a freed child the whole camp has silently agreed to protect.
- **The Veiled One**, a mage the Legion needs and fears, because no one is sure
  whether the magic is the gentle kind or the kind that kills the ground.

### Enemies & pressures (GM may advance)

- A templar huntmaster has been sent from the city to retake "stolen property."
- Water and heat grind the Legion down a little every day.
- A faction wants to stop running and raid for a stronghold; another wants to
  slip away to a rumored haven. The split is widening.

### What it needs that cannot be bought

Legitimacy and water security — a place and a claim that make the Free Legion a
people the world must reckon with, not a mob to be hunted down.
