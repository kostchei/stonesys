# Steading: The Hollow-Oak Holt

Setting: Elfquest playset. Original, unofficial adaptation.

A Bastion sheet (see `core.md`) for the shared home of the Elfquest playset: a
small tribe of forest elves who live in and around a vast hollow oak, pressed by
a hardening world — a longer cold each winter, humans pushing at the wood's
edge, and game growing scarce. Of late they have taken in an outsider or two.

Note: this playset uses the **village** embedment style. There are no Patron,
God, or Bastion power tracks on the playbooks; ties are personal, carried in
Bonds, and the elves' magic lives in their moves. This steading sheet is the
tribe's shared object; the elves' bond to it is the whole point of every
playbook's community tie.

## Bastion Sheet

- **Name:** The Hollow-Oak Holt.
- **Location:** deep in an old forest, sheltered in a fold of hills around a
  single enormous, ancient hollow oak.
- **Look:** dens and platforms grown and woven into the great tree and the wood
  around it, wolf-trails threading out to the hunting grounds, smoke curling
  from the Mother Tree's heart.
- **Founder / owner:** the tribe entire; led by whoever holds the chief's lodge,
  and guided by the eldest and the shapers.
- **Purpose:** keep the tribe fed, whole, and hidden, and hold the old ways
  against a world that is turning colder and more crowded.
- **Scale:** `0`  ·  **Safety:** `+1`  ·  **Prosperity:** `0`  ·  **Influence:** `-1`  ·  **Supply:** `+1`

### Facilities

1. **The Mother Tree.** Function: shelter and heart of the Holt, where the tribe
   gathers, sleeps, and keeps its stores. Rating used: Safety. Does especially
   well: hiding and warming the whole tribe through the worst weather.
   Vulnerability: it is wood, and fire is the one thing it cannot forgive.
2. **The Shaping-Grove.** Function: where the shapers work wood, stone, and flesh
   — the tribe's craft, healing, and quiet magic. Rating used: Supply. Does
   especially well: mending the hurt and making what the tribe needs from the
   living land. Vulnerability: it leans on a very few gifted hands; lose one and
   much goes undone.

### Trouble

`1` — the last winter ran long, the game is thin this season, and a human
hunter was seen too near the wood's edge.

### The old magic in the land

The Holt sits where the world's deep, old magic still runs close to the surface
— in the Mother Tree, in the shaped stone, in the elves themselves.

- Visible sign: shaped wood and rock that no human hand could make; wolves that
  are more than wolves; the way the tribe's shapers simply know things.
- Scarcity: it cannot be mined or hoarded, only lived with and tended.
- Side effect: it draws the notice of humans (as devilry) and of older things
  that remember when the magic was stronger.
- Who wants it: humans who fear or covet it, trolls who resent it, and the tribe
  itself, which is slowly forgetting how much it has lost.
- What happens as it fades: each generation shapes a little less, remembers a
  little less, and the cold presses a little closer.

### People (community-tie hooks)

- **Eldest Marrowen**, who remembers more than anyone and is not sure how much
  longer she can carry it.
- **Kest**, a proud hunter who thinks the tribe should drive the humans off
  rather than hide, and chafes at every soft word.
- **Little Fenn**, a cub too young for the hunt, curious about everything and
  everyone the tribe brings home.
- **Grethka**, a troll who came to the Holt as an outsider and stayed; proof that
  strangers can become kin, and a reminder of how hard that is.

### Enemies & pressures (GM may advance)

- The winters lengthen; the game thins; starvation is a slow, patient enemy.
- Humans push at the forest's edge, fearful and armed, and one has seen too much.
- Trolls in the deep hills covet the Holt's shaped stone and old grudges.
- The tribe's magic and memory fade a little with each hard year.

### What it cannot buy

A safe hunting range as the cold deepens, peace with the humans at the wood's
edge, or the return of the ones the long winters have already taken.

## Source trail

Built from general, public knowledge of the Elfquest milieu (forest-dwelling
elves, wolf-bonds, silent "sending," soul-deep "Recognition," and shaping magics
of flesh, plant, and stone, set against a harsh world and wary humans). All
names, the steading, and all text above are original to this file.
