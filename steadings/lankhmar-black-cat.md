# Steading: The Sign of the Black Cat

Setting: Lankhmar playset. Original, unofficial adaptation.

A Bastion sheet (see `core.md`) for the shared home of the Lankhmar playset: a
band of ne'er-do-wells working out of a rented cellar in a poor quarter of a
great, fog-choked, god-ridden city, scraping a living between guild dues, the
watch, rival gangs, and the whims of a hundred small gods.

## Bastion Sheet

- **Name:** The Sign of the Black Cat — a cellar under a failing wine-shop whose
  faded sign gives the band its name.
- **Location:** three steps down off a crooked lane in the poorest, oldest
  quarter, where the fog never fully lifts and the watch never fully enters.
- **Look:** a low, smoke-stained room of mismatched chairs and a good strong
  door, a bolt-hole behind the wine-casks, and a black cat that no one admits to
  feeding.
- **Founder / owner:** held together, barely, by whoever paid the rent last and
  whoever the others have not yet decided to knife.
- **Purpose:** stay fed, stay unhanged, and pull off the one score that buys a
  better life — or at least a better cellar.
- **Scale:** `-1`  ·  **Safety:** `+1`  ·  **Prosperity:** `0`  ·  **Influence:** `+1`  ·  **Supply:** `0`

### Facilities

1. **The Cellar-Den.** Function: hide people, loot, and secrets; lie low when the
   watch or the guild comes calling. Rating used: Safety. Does especially well:
   making a person or a thing disappear for a while. Vulnerability: it is one
   betrayed door away from a trap with no way out.
2. **The Fence's Corner.** Function: turn stolen goods into coin and coin into
   favors. Rating used: Prosperity. Does especially well: moving hot goods
   quietly. Vulnerability: every sale leaves a thread the Thieves' Guild can pull.

### Trouble

`1` — the rent is late, the guild dues are later, and someone in the band has
been talking where they shouldn't.

### The Underway (the den's secret)

Beneath the cellar, a forgotten branch of the city's ancient drains and
smugglers' tunnels — a way under half the quarter, known to almost no one.

- Visible sign: a damp draft behind the casks, and rats that come and go with
  purpose.
- Scarcity: the map of it lives in one scavenger's head and nowhere else.
- Side effect: what uses the Underway does not only run one direction.
- Who wants it: a rival gang, the Thieves' Guild, and something older below.
- When it's lost: the den becomes just a cellar with one door.

### People (community-tie hooks)

- **Old Sabra**, who keeps the den, pays the rent, and remembers every favor
  owed in the quarter.
- **Feathers**, the guild's soft-spoken collector, who is friendly right up until
  he isn't.
- **Brother Gnarl**, a gutter-priest of a small, spiteful god, who is right about
  omens just often enough to be frightening.
- **Wren**, a lookout too young for this life, whom the whole band silently
  shields.

### Enemies & pressures (GM may advance)

- The Thieves' Guild expects its cut and its obedience; fall behind and you are
  declared unsanctioned, which in this city is a death sentence.
- The Overlord's watch and a rival gang both covet the quarter.
- The small gods of the city are real, near, and easily offended.
- The fog, the plague-seasons, and Death itself walk these lanes.

### What it cannot buy

A true patron's protection, real trust among themselves, or the one clean score
that lifts the band out of the cellar before the cellar becomes a grave.

## Source trail

Built from general, public knowledge of the sword-and-sorcery Lankhmar milieu
(a corrupt fog-bound city, thieves' and assassins' guilds, a street of many
small gods, ever-present Death). All names, the steading, and all text above are
original to this file.
