# StoneSys Core Rules

Research date: 2026-06-29

This file contains original StoneSys rules modules. The inspiration targets for these sections are:

- Shadowdark: fast procedures, dangerous bargains, treasure-fueled downtime, divine risk.
- Forgotten Realms: active factions, named powers, public religion, temples, guilds, orders, patron networks.
- Lankhmar: street-level patrons, corrupt institutions, capricious gods, debts, favors, and urban consequences.
- Red Steel: frontier strongholds, strange resources, dangerous prosperity, regional pressure, and magical contamination.

## Patrons

Patrons are powerful backers who can help a character, party, village, or bastion. A patron is not necessarily divine. A patron might be a noble house, thieves' guild, merchant cartel, temple, hidden master, wizard, crime lord, military order, ancient spirit, dragon, demon, revolutionary cell, or distant empire.

Patrons exist to make power transactional. They give access, protection, favors, secrets, magic, work, and trouble.

### Patron Sheet

Every patron has:

- Name
- Type
- Agenda
- Public face
- Hidden want
- Gift
- Price
- Taboo
- Enemies
- Current demand
- Bond
- Debt
- Heat

### Bond

Bond measures how reliable the relationship is.

- `0`: Known contact. The patron recognizes you.
- `1`: Useful asset. The patron will spend minor resources on you.
- `2`: Trusted agent. The patron gives serious aid and sensitive work.
- `3`: Marked favorite. The patron treats your success as part of their own power.

Raise Bond when you complete a patron demand, protect the patron's interests at real cost, bring them rare treasure, or expand their influence.

Lower Bond when you refuse a demand, embarrass the patron, aid a rival, break a taboo, or fail in a way that damages their agenda.

### Debt

Debt measures what the patron can call in.

Debt starts at `0` and can rise to `6`. When Debt reaches `6`, the patron makes a major demand immediately and Debt resets to `3` after the demand is accepted or resolved.

Add Debt when:

- You ask for help without paying now.
- You invoke a patron in danger.
- You use the patron's name to gain access.
- You fail a patron move.
- You keep a gift after refusing its price.

Clear Debt when:

- You complete a demand.
- You pay a costly tithe, secret, service, hostage, oath, or treasure.
- You accept lasting fallout on the patron's behalf.

### Heat

Heat measures attention from rivals, authorities, enemies, and suspicious locals.

- `0`: Quiet.
- `1`: Rumors.
- `2`: Watched.
- `3`: Targeted.
- `4`: Open retaliation.

Heat rises when patron business becomes public, when the party acts violently or dramatically, or when rivals are provoked. Heat falls through time, bribes, diplomacy, scapegoats, or decisive victories.

### Invoke Patron

When you ask a patron for immediate aid, say what you want and what leverage you have. Then roll `2d6 + Bond - Debt risk`.

Debt risk is `0` if the request is modest, `-1` if the request is dangerous, and `-2` if the request could expose or weaken the patron.

On a `10+`, choose two:

- The aid arrives quickly.
- The aid is exactly what you asked for.
- No new Debt is added.
- Heat does not rise.
- The patron reveals useful extra information.

On a `7-9`, choose one from the list above. The GM also chooses one:

- Add `1 Debt`.
- Add `1 Heat`.
- The aid is partial, delayed, or compromised.
- The patron attaches a demand.
- A rival learns about the request.

On a `6-`, the patron still answers if it is interesting, but the GM makes a hard move. Add `1 Debt` and `1 Heat`, or mark the patron as offended.

### Patron Favors

Common favors include:

- Shelter
- Legal cover
- Forged papers
- Rumors
- Safe passage
- A specialist hireling
- A squad of rough allies
- A minor magic item
- A curse lifted temporarily
- A door opened in high society
- A rival distracted
- A secret revealed
- A bastion facility supported for one turn

### Patron Demands

When Debt comes due, choose or roll:

1. Recover a stolen object.
2. Silence a witness.
3. Protect a shipment.
4. Spy on a rival.
5. Escort a dangerous guest.
6. Break a taboo to prove loyalty.
7. Deliver tribute to a shrine, guild, or court.
8. Publicly endorse the patron.
9. Sabotage a rival's bastion or project.
10. Hide someone who is being hunted.
11. Enter a ruin for something the patron will not name.
12. Accept a mark, oath, geas, brand, or visible token.

### Patron Types

Use these as templates.

| Type | Gift | Price |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Noble House | Status, soldiers, legal reach | Reputation, obedience, marriage, taxes |
| Thieves' Guild | Fences, spies, safehouses | Cuts, silence, underworld loyalty |
| Temple | Healing, sanctuary, relic access | Tithes, doctrine, public service |
| Arcane Master | Spells, lore, strange tools | Experiments, secrets, corruption |
| Merchant League | Credit, cargo, maps | Contracts, monopolies, debt |
| Warlord | Troops, weapons, intimidation | Violence, conquest, tribute |
| Ancient Spirit | Omens, land magic, protection | Rites, taboos, sacrifice |
| Outsider | Impossible aid, curses, visions | Soul marks, bargains, future claims |

## Gods

Gods are powers of worship, fear, place, and portfolio. They are more public than patrons and more dangerous to treat casually. A god can be a true deity, ancestor host, saint, demon-god, local river power, dead empire's idol, cosmic principle, or city cult.

Gods exist to make belief matter at the table.

### God Sheet

Every god has:

- Name
- Titles
- Portfolio
- Symbols
- Holy places
- Tenets
- Taboos
- Favored offerings
- Common worshippers
- Priesthood style
- Rival powers
- Omens
- Wrath

### Devotion

Devotion measures a character's standing with a god.

- `0`: Occasional worshipper.
- `1`: Sworn faithful.
- `2`: Favored servant.
- `3`: Chosen instrument.

Gain Devotion when you uphold a tenet at cost, complete a holy task, make a meaningful offering, protect the god's people, restore a shrine, or defeat a rival power.

Lose Devotion when you break a taboo, profane a holy place, ignore a direct omen, aid a hated rival, or use divine gifts selfishly.

### Favor

Favor is a spendable divine hold. You can hold up to `3 Favor` from one god at a time.

Gain `1 Favor` when you complete a rite, sacrifice treasure, fulfill a vow, or roll a strong hit on a prayer.

Spend `1 Favor` to:

- Add `+1` to a roll that clearly serves the god's portfolio.
- Ask the GM for an omen.
- Turn a weak hit on a holy act into a stronger result.
- Protect an ally from a minor curse, fear, or spiritual harm.
- Declare that a small religious contact, shrine, or sign exists nearby.

### Pray For Aid

When you pray for divine aid under pressure, name the god, state the request, and offer something meaningful. Roll `2d6 + Devotion`.

On a `10+`, the god grants a clear sign or useful intervention. Choose one:

- Healing or relief from a condition.
- Protection from a specific danger.
- Revelation of a hidden truth.
- A weapon, tool, or spell empowered for one scene.
- A hostile spirit, undead, curse, or demon forced back.
- A path opened where none seemed available.

On a `7-9`, the aid comes with a condition. Choose one:

- Mark `1 Favor` owed as a vow.
- The effect is brief or incomplete.
- The god demands visible obedience now.
- A rival power notices.
- The answer is symbolic and must be interpreted.

On a `6-`, the god is silent, offended, or terrifyingly present. The GM makes a hard move and may mark Wrath.

### Wrath

Wrath measures divine displeasure.

- `0`: No wrath.
- `1`: Bad omens.
- `2`: Blessings fail or become unreliable.
- `3`: The god sends agents, dreams, curses, or monsters.
- `4`: The character is excommunicated, cursed, hunted, or spiritually marked.

Reduce Wrath through confession, restitution, pilgrimage, sacrifice, completing a hard command, or making peace with the offended power.

### Portfolios

Use portfolios to define what a god can easily influence. A god can have one to three major portfolios.

Examples:

- Dawn and renewal
- Death and burial
- Storms and sea
- Hearth and oaths
- Trade and roads
- Secrets and thieves
- War and conquest
- Mercy and endurance
- Craft and invention
- Forests and beasts
- Fate and prophecy
- Fire and purification
- Night and loss
- Justice and watchfulness

### Everyday Religion

Most people honor many gods. A farmer might pray to rain, sun, hearth, roads, birth, and death in the same month. Exclusive devotion is for priests, zealots, chosen heroes, oathbound orders, and the desperate.

This matters mechanically:

- Anyone can make offerings and receive omens.
- Priests and chosen servants gain Devotion faster.
- Breaking a local god's taboo can matter even if the character follows another god.
- Rival gods can both have legitimate claims on the same character.

### Divine Offerings

Offerings should cost something.

| Offering | Usual Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Coin, gems, or treasure | Gain Favor if the amount matters to the character |
| Food, wine, incense, or candles | Seek an omen or minor blessing |
| Blood, pain, fasting, or ordeal | Ask for protection, cleansing, or power |
| Vow, oath, or public service | Gain Devotion if fulfilled |
| Relic, enemy trophy, or reclaimed shrine | Reduce Wrath or raise Devotion |

## Bastions

Bastions are player-controlled bases with rules weight. A bastion might be a village hall, tower, shrine, mine, dockhouse, guild den, fortified farm, caravanserai, hidden cellar, border keep, ship, ruined manor, or frontier settlement.

Bastions exist to make home, logistics, followers, territory, and downtime matter.

### Bastion Sheet

Every bastion has:

- Name
- Location
- Look
- Founder or owner
- Purpose
- Scale
- Safety
- Prosperity
- Influence
- Supply
- Trouble
- Facilities
- Hirelings
- Patrons
- Enemies
- Strange resource, if any

### Bastion Ratings

Each rating runs from `-1` to `+3`.

- Scale: size, reach, and population.
- Safety: walls, guards, secrecy, law, and defensibility.
- Prosperity: money, trade, harvest, craft, and comfort.
- Influence: reputation, political reach, faith, fear, and alliances.
- Supply: stores, tools, food, medicine, and repair capacity.

At creation, assign these ratings: `+1`, `+1`, `0`, `0`, `-1`.

### Facilities

A bastion begins with two facilities. Add one facility when the bastion improves Scale, receives major investment, completes a construction project, or claims a meaningful site.

Common facilities:

- Barracks
- Shrine
- Workshop
- Library
- Infirmary
- Watchtower
- Market
- Garden
- Stables
- Dock
- Vault
- Guildhall
- Laboratory
- Tavern
- Courtroom
- Hidden room
- Mine
- Foundry
- Signal tower
- Training yard

Each facility has:

- Function
- Rating used
- One order it performs especially well
- One vulnerability

### Bastion Turns

Take a Bastion Turn when a week passes, after an expedition, during downtime, or when the GM says the wider world has moved.

On a Bastion Turn:

1. Choose one order for the bastion.
2. Choose one facility to lead the order.
3. Roll `2d6 + the relevant bastion rating`.
4. Apply the result.
5. The GM advances one external pressure.

On a `10+`, the order succeeds cleanly. Choose one extra benefit.

On a `7-9`, the order succeeds, but choose one cost.

On a `6-`, the order fails or succeeds at a serious price. The GM makes a hard move against the bastion.

### Bastion Orders

| Order | Rating | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Build | Supply | Add, repair, or upgrade a facility |
| Craft | Prosperity | Produce gear, tools, medicine, or trade goods |
| Gather | Supply | Stock food, water, fuel, herbs, ore, or other resources |
| Recruit | Influence | Attract hirelings, guards, specialists, or settlers |
| Research | Influence or Prosperity | Learn lore, maps, weaknesses, rituals, or formulas |
| Trade | Prosperity | Convert goods into coin, favors, or rare supplies |
| Patrol | Safety | Reduce danger, scout threats, or protect roads |
| Hide | Safety | Conceal people, treasure, fugitives, or forbidden work |
| Heal | Supply | Recover wounds, debilities, disease, curses, or morale |
| Celebrate | Influence | Reduce unrest, gain rumors, or create bonds |
| Petition | Influence | Ask a patron, god, court, guild, or faction for support |

### Extra Benefits

On a strong hit for a Bastion Order, choose one:

- Gain `1 Supply`.
- Gain `1 Coin`.
- Gain `1 Favor` with a patron, god, or faction.
- Reduce Heat, Trouble, or Wrath by `1`.
- Complete the work faster than expected.
- A hireling, neighbor, or ally improves.
- Learn a useful secret.
- Create an opening for the next adventure.

### Costs

On a weak hit, choose one:

- Spend `1 Supply`.
- Spend `1 Coin`.
- Add `1 Trouble`.
- Add `1 Heat`.
- A facility is strained and cannot lead the next Bastion Turn.
- A hireling is injured, offended, missing, or compromised.
- A patron demands credit or payment.
- A rival copies, blocks, or exploits the work.

### Trouble

Trouble measures internal and external pressure on the bastion.

- `0`: Stable.
- `1`: Complaints, shortages, rumors.
- `2`: Open problem.
- `3`: Crisis.
- `4`: Disaster.

At Trouble `4`, the GM makes a major bastion move and resets Trouble to `2` after the crisis changes the situation.

### Bastion Moves For The GM

Use these when a Bastion Turn misses, when Trouble reaches `4`, or when the world pushes back.

- A faction makes a claim.
- A god sends an omen.
- A patron calls in Debt.
- A hireling betrays, quits, or asks for help.
- Supplies spoil, vanish, or are stolen.
- A monster, army, gang, or official arrives.
- A facility is damaged.
- A strange resource flares, corrupts, or runs dry.
- A neighbor asks for protection.
- A rival offers a tempting alliance.
- The bastion's success attracts settlers and enemies.
- The land itself changes.

### Frontier Resources

Some bastions control a strange resource. This is especially appropriate for borderlands, mines, cursed coasts, fallen empires, and magically scarred regions.

A strange resource has:

- Name
- Use
- Visible sign
- Scarcity
- Side effect
- Who wants it
- What happens when it runs out

Examples:

- Blood-red ore that strengthens weapons but sickens workers.
- Blue salt that preserves food and memories.
- Glassy stone that stores spells but draws spirits.
- Silver-black oil that burns underwater.
- Amber resin that heals wounds but causes prophetic fevers.
- Meteor iron that wounds monsters but attracts star cults.

When a bastion exploits a strange resource, add `+1 Prosperity` while the resource flows, but roll for a side effect whenever the bastion rolls a miss on Gather, Craft, Trade, or Build.

### Resource Side Effects

Choose or roll:

1. Workers develop marks, mutations, dreams, or compulsions.
2. A faction demands exclusive rights.
3. A god, spirit, or monster claims the resource as sacred.
4. The resource changes a facility.
5. Local animals, crops, or weather become strange.
6. A cure, stabilizer, or protective charm becomes necessary.
7. Smugglers and raiders arrive.
8. The resource empowers enemies as easily as allies.
9. The supply is richer but more dangerous than expected.
10. The resource awakens something below.

### Bastions And The Party

A bastion should create adventures, not replace them. If the bastion can solve a problem alone, let it. If it cannot, the Bastion Turn should reveal what the characters must do personally.

Good bastion questions:

- Who feels safer because this place exists?
- Who feels threatened by it?
- What does it need that cannot be bought?
- Which patron wants influence here?
- Which god is worshipped here?
- What secret is buried under it?
- What resource makes it worth attacking?
- What would the characters sacrifice to keep it?
