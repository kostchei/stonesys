# The Dice

This game uses six kinds of dice. Three are **positive** — they work for you.
Three are **negative** — they work against you. Every roll throws a handful
of both, together, and reads all of them at once.

| Die | Color | Sides | What it represents |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Ability** | green | d8 | your raw capability — the stat you're rolling |
| **Proficiency** | yellow | d12 | trained mastery; the only die that can show **◆ Triumph** |
| **Boost** | blue | d6 | a momentary edge: the right tool, high ground, a friend's help |
| **Difficulty** | purple | d8 | how hard the task is |
| **Challenge** | red | d12 | real peril; the only die that can show **✶ Despair** |
| **Setback** | black | d6 | a momentary hindrance: bad light, no footing, being watched |

You never add numbers on these dice. Their faces show **symbols**, and
symbols are all that matter.

## The symbols

There are four pairs. Within the first two pairs, opposites cancel each other
one for one.

- **✱ Success** (on green, yellow, and blue dice) and **☓ Failure** (on
  purple, red, and black dice). Each ☓ cancels one ✱. If at least one ✱
  survives, the task **works**. Otherwise it doesn't.
- **▲ Advantage** (positive dice) and **▼ Threat** (negative dice). Each ▼
  cancels one ▲. Whatever survives is the roll's *side effect* — good
  fortune around the edges of the task, or cost and complication. Advantage
  and threat are completely independent of success and failure: you can
  succeed and still owe a price, or fail and still catch a break.
- **◆ Triumph** appears only on the yellow die. It counts as one ✱ toward
  success, *and* it means something remarkable breaks your way — above and
  beyond the task at hand.
- **✶ Despair** appears only on the red die. It counts as one ☓ toward
  failure, *and* it means something goes memorably, dramatically wrong.

**◆ and ✶ are never cancelled.** Their success/failure contribution cancels
normally, but the triumph or despair itself always fires. A roll can succeed
and still contain a ✶; a roll can fail and still contain a ◆. When both
appear on one roll, both happen.

## Reading a handful

Roll every die in the pool together. Then:

1. Count surviving ✱ after cancellation. One or more = the task worked.
2. Count surviving ▲ or ▼ after cancellation. That's the side effect.
3. Note every ◆ and ✶. Each one fires, no matter what else the dice say.

That's the whole engine. Chapter 4 tells you which dice to pick up; chapter
5 tells you what the results mean at the table.
