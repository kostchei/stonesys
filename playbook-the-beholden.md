# Playbook: The Beholden

Research/design date: 2026-06-30

This is a worked example playbook for StoneSys. It is built on the locked
character chassis and demonstrates the three-layer playsheet structure
(Archetype, Embedment, Levers). It foregrounds the **Patron** system from
`core.md`, so the Layer 2 embedment is mechanically live rather than flavor.

## Chassis This Playbook Assumes

- Stats: STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA, rated `1-5`.
- Roll modifier: `stat - 2` (range `-1` to `+3`).
- Resolution: `2d6 + mod`. `10+` strong hit, `7-9` cost or choice, `6-` miss (+XP).
- Starting array: `{4, 3, 3, 2, 2, 1}`, archetype sets the default assignment,
  player may swap two values. Stat cap `5`.
- Power tracks (Bond, Debt, Heat, Devotion, Favor, Wrath) come from `core.md`
  and keep their own `0-3` / `0-6` scales. They are not characteristics.

---

## Concept

You were no one. A power lifted you out of the gutter, the ledger, or the
grave, and made you useful. Now their reach is your reach, and their leash is
always around your throat. You can open doors no one else can, because someone
above you allows it. For now.

The Beholden matters to the community because a patron's eyes are on it through
you, whether the community knows it or not.

## LAYER 1 — Archetype

### Names

Sera, Corin, Vesh, Oda, Lira, Tam, Brannoc, Mire, Sable, Joss, Halle, Pike,
Wenna, Dray, Castor. (Choose one or your own.)

### Look (choose one per line)

- **Bearing:** watchful / ingratiating / cold / restless
- **Dress:** borrowed finery / practical leathers / a patron's livery / deliberately plain
- **Eyes:** calculating / tired / hungry / always hidden
- **Mark:** a patron's token worn openly / a brand or tattoo you keep covered / a fine scar / nothing you will show

### Stats (default assignment)

Assign the array `{4, 3, 3, 2, 2, 1}` as printed, then **swap any two**:

| CHA | DEX | INT | WIS | CON | STR |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 4   | 3   | 3   | 2   | 2   | 1   |

- **Hard anchor:** your CHA is `3` or higher. The Beholden lives by leverage.

## LAYER 3 — Levers

### Derived

- **HP:** `16 + CON`.
- **Damage die:** `d6`.
- **Load:** `6 + STR`.

### Starting gear (choose one per menu)

- **Tool of the trade:** a fine blade (`d6`) / a brace of knives (`d6`, thrown) / a walking cane that is also a club (`d6`)
- **Cover:** merchant's papers / a healer's kit / a minstrel's instrument
- **Insurance:** a purse of someone else's coin / a folio of useful secrets / a single favor token from your patron

### Signature move (always on)

**Strings.** You have favors, debts, and dirt scattered across your patron's
reach. When you arrive somewhere within that reach and quietly put out feelers
for a contact, roll `2d6 + (CHA-2)`.

- `10+`: hold 3 Strings.
- `7-9`: hold 1 String.
- `6-`: you still find someone, but the GM holds the Strings against you.

Spend 1 String to declare one of the following true, now:

- A useful contact is already here and willing to talk.
- Someone nearby owes you a small debt you can call in.
- You know a back way, a safe room, or a quiet exit.
- You produce a token of passage, forged or genuine.

Any String you spend by leaning on the patron's name adds `1 Heat`.

### Leash (always on)

When you **Invoke Patron** (`core.md`), you may take `+1 Debt` to treat a `6-`
as a `7-9`, or a `7-9` as a `10+`. The patron remembers every indulgence.

### Choose 2 of these moves

1. **Read the Room.** When you enter a charged situation and size it up, roll
   `2d6 + (WIS-2)`. `10+` ask 3, `7-9` ask 1: Who here is afraid? Who holds the
   real power? Who owes whom? Where is the quickest way out? Take `+1 forward`
   acting on the answers.
2. **A Face for Every Door.** When you pass yourself off as someone you are not,
   roll `2d6 + (CHA-2)`. `10+` they buy it until you give them a hard reason not
   to. `7-9` they buy it for now, but the GM names the catch.
3. **Collateral.** When you put up something of value as surety to seal a deal,
   take `+1 ongoing` to that deal while the collateral stands. If you forfeit it,
   mark XP and add `1 Heat`.
4. **Eyes Everywhere.** When you spend a quiet day in a settlement listening,
   roll `2d6 + (INT-2)`. `10+` hold 2, `7-9` hold 1. Spend a hold to learn a
   secret currently in play there.
5. **The Patron's Purse.** Once per session, declare that your patron staked
   you: gain coin or a needed common item now, and add `1 Debt`.
6. **Hard to Kill.** When you would take a fatal blow, you may add `1 Debt` and
   survive instead, visibly marked by how your patron reached out to save you.

### Advancement

Mark XP on a miss and when a move says so. Spend XP to take one option per level
(stats max `5`):

- `+1` to a stat.
- `+1` to a stat.
- Take another Beholden move.
- Take another Beholden move.
- Take a move from another playbook.
- Take a move from another playbook.
- Gain a second patron (new Patron Sheet, Bond `0`).
- Increase your maximum held Strings by `1`.
- Once per session, your Leash costs no Debt.
- Buy a piece of yourself back: reduce standing Debt by `1`, permanently.

### Advanced advancement (after 5 advances)

- **Cut the Cord.** End your bond with a patron for good, resolving the fallout
  in play. Choose a new embedment: claim or found a Bastion, or take up a god
  (start Devotion `1`).
- **Become a Patron.** Create a small Patron Sheet with you as the gift-giver;
  others may now Invoke you.
- **Walk Away.** Retire to safety, or take up the mantle your patron leaves
  behind.

## LAYER 2 — Embedment

This is the part that makes a Beholden a Beholden rather than a generic rogue.
Resolve it at creation.

### Power-structure embedment (the differentiator)

The Beholden foregrounds **exactly one** of the three power systems: the
**Patron**.

- Define a patron now using the Patron Sheet in `core.md`.
- Start with **Bond `1`** and **Debt `2`** with that patron.
- You begin with no Devotion and no Bastion standing. Those are earned in play,
  if ever.

### Bonds (resolve 1-2 at creation)

- `____` knew me before my patron did. They remember who I was.
- `____` has done work for me that could get them hanged, and I hold it gently.
- I owe `____` a debt that has nothing to do with my patron, and it galls me.
- `____` thinks they can get me free of my patron. They are wrong, but I let
  them believe it.
- `____` is the only one I would warn before my patron came calling.

### Community tie (choose 1, name the NPC)

- Your patron wants a foothold in the home. `____`, who lives there, does not
  know you are the wedge.
- The home took you in when your patron once cast you off. You would protect
  `____` there even against orders.
- You launder your patron's interests through `____`'s business in the home.

### Duty line (fixed)

Your power is borrowed. Everything you can do, you can do because someone above
you allows it. For now.

## The Nudge, Demonstrated

The default assignment is a face-and-footwork fixer (CHA 4, DEX/INT 3). A player
who wants a harder, more durable Beholden can **swap two values** — but the
`CHA 3+` anchor stops them from abandoning the archetype entirely.

An illegal nudge, trying to build a mute bruiser:

> Swap CHA and STR:
> **STR 4 · DEX 3 · INT 3 · WIS 2 · CON 2 · CHA 1** — *violates the `CHA 3+` anchor.*

The anchor rejects it: a Beholden with no leverage is not a Beholden. A legal
nudge toward durability instead:

> Swap DEX and CON:
> **CHA 4 · CON 3 · INT 3 · WIS 2 · DEX 2 · STR 1**

Same six numbers, same balanced budget, a Beholden who can take a hit and still
talk their way out. The archetype set the shape; the player nudged it within the
guardrail.
