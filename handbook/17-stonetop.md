# 17. Stonetop

## The premise

A small farming village stands alone at the edge of the known world, under a
great standing stone, a hard week from anywhere. Your characters are not
visitors — they are the village's own: its keeper of old rites, its sheriff,
its huntress, its restless youth. The wilds around are old and haunted, the
neighbors are far and not all friendly, and every year the village either
grows a little stronger or slips a little closer to the dark. This is hearth
fantasy: the adventure matters because home does.

*A note on this chapter.* Stonetop's nine playbooks are drawn from a
published game that this campaign converts for table use. Out of respect for
that work, this chapter does not reproduce the playbooks' text. What follows
are **conversion profiles**: everything you need to run each archetype on
this engine — its numbers, its shape, and how its moves translate — written
in this book's own words. Play with the converted sheets (the web sheets
carry the full detail), and with a copy of the original game on the shelf it
came from.

## Your home

The village is the campaign's steading (chapter 15). Set it up with the GM
at the table: its ratings are those of a small, remote, self-reliant farming
settlement — modest Scale, decent Supply, low Influence — with facilities
along the lines of a shrine to the local goddess, a common hall, a smithy,
and a palisade. Its Fortunes carry the year; its Surplus feeds the winter;
its Trouble is never far. The old roads, the ruins beneath and beyond, and
the village's two distant neighbor-settlements — a marsh trade-town and a
mining camp — supply a steady stream of expeditions of all three kinds
(chapter 16).

## Embedment style

Full suite. Stonetop playbooks use the village's gods (chapter 13 — the
local earth-mother faith and the shrine-keeping that goes with it), patrons
where the fiction offers them (chapter 12), Standing with the village, and
bonds and community ties throughout. The steading is the campaign's heart:
expect season moves and steading turns every few sessions.

## Converting the playbooks

Every move on the original playbooks converts by the standard map, which the
GM applies on the fly wherever a sheet hasn't already been converted:

- "Roll plus a stat" → build a pool on that stat (chapter 4), trained if
  you have the move. Strong hit → **success with ▲**; weak hit → **success
  with ▼** (or clean); miss → **failure, mark XP**.
- "Take +1 forward / ongoing" → **+1 blue die** on that roll / while the
  fiction holds. Disadvantage → **+1 black die**.
- "Hold 1 / spend hold" → identical here (chapter 9), earned via ▲ menus.
- Uses, charges, and stock-pouches → a named **hold** refilled by rite,
  forage, or rest as the move describes.
- ◆ and ✶ have no old equivalent: on ◆, read the move's most generous
  line and then some; on ✶, its worst outcome arrives immediately.

## The nine playbooks

### The Blessed

**HP 20 · damage d6.** The village's link to the old faith and the spirits
of the land — herb-lore, rites, and the slow bargains of the wild. Anchor
stat WIS. Backgrounds shape the role: an *initiate* of the village's sacred
order (with fellow initiates as followers), one *raised in the wild* among
beasts and trees, or a *vessel* of the goddess herself. Signature work is
spirit-craft: speaking with what lives in the land and calling on it, fueled
by a pouch of sacred stock — a hold spent on veils, wards, blessings, and
borrowed powers. Plays as the party's spiritual anchor and the steading's
conscience.

### The Fox

**HP 20 · damage d6.** The charming rogue the village never quite reforms —
light fingers, lighter feet, and a friend (or mark) in every settlement on
the road. Anchor DEX/CHA. Backgrounds: a *natural-born talent*, a veteran of
a *criminal life* now half-retired, or the *prodigal* lately returned with a
past in tow. Signature work is misdirection: passing yourself off, lifting
what isn't nailed down, and talking the trouble that follows into someone
else's lap — with a hold of leverage over people who should have known
better. Plays as the party's face and problem-solver, and its problem.

### The Heavy

**HP 24 · damage d10.** The village's wall: the strongest arm in the
settlement and the one who stands in front. Anchor STR. Backgrounds: the
*sheriff* keeping the peace, a fighter with a *blood-soaked past* trying to
bury it, or one *marked by the storm* with something uncanny in the blood.
Signature work is violence made deliberate — threats that end fights before
they start, and fights that end fast when they must. The biggest HP pool and
damage die in the set. Plays as the party's protector and its blunt
instrument.

### The Judge

**HP 20 · damage d6.** Keeper of the village's law and its chronicle,
speaker of hard truths with an old book and an older mace. Anchor WIS/CHA.
Backgrounds: heir to a *legacy* of judges, a *missionary* carrying the faith
outward, or a *prophet* who hears more than is comfortable. Signature work
is authority: invoking the law, weighing testimony, and calling the faithful
to account under the village's god of light and order. Plays as the party's
compass — and the one who must rule when neighbors fall out.

### The Lightbearer

**HP 18 · damage d6.** Bearer of the holy flame, sworn against the things
that move in the dark. Anchor WIS. Backgrounds: an *auspicious birth* under
signs, a *wandering mystic* come to rest, or a *soul on fire* remade by
revelation. Signature work is the light itself: kindling sacred flame that
reveals, blesses, and burns the unnatural, fueled by devotion and rite
(chapter 13 does heavy duty here). Fragile in the body, radiant in the
scene. Plays as the party's lantern against everything the wilds hide.

### The Marshal

**HP 20 · damage d8.** The one who raises the village's fighters and leads
them — drill, watch-rosters, and the weight of every name on the muster.
Anchor CHA/STR. Backgrounds: *scion* of a martial line, a *penitent* paying
for an old defeat, or a *luminary* whose deeds already carry. Signature work
is command: a crew of followers who hold, flank, and rally as ordered — a
hold of discipline spent to make ordinary people brave. Plays as the force
multiplier; the Marshal turns the steading's Safety rating into people.

### The Ranger

**HP 18 · damage d8.** The village's eyes beyond the fields — tracker,
archer, and reader of the deep wood. Anchor DEX/WIS. Backgrounds: a *mighty
hunter* of great beasts, a *wide wanderer* who knows the far roads, or one
*bonded to a beast* who scouts at their side. Signature work is the hunt:
marking a quarry and closing on it with a hold that makes the wild an ally
— shortcuts, sign, ambush ground. Plays as the party's guide, and the
reason expeditions arrive where they meant to.

### The Seeker

**HP 16 · damage d6.** The one who digs where the village would rather not
look — ruins, relics, and the sealed places of the makers who came before.
Anchor INT. Backgrounds: a *patriot* seeking power for home, an
*antiquarian* who loves the things themselves, or a *witch-hunter* tracking
what leaks from below. Signature work is study: unlocking what a found
thing is and what it costs, with a hold of insight into the old world —
and a bad habit of bringing its attention home. Plays as the party's
scholar and the campaign's trouble-magnet.

### The Would-Be Hero

**HP 16 · damage d6.** Young, unproven, and absolutely certain — the one
with more nerve than sense and a destiny nobody else has noticed yet.
Anchor varies with the dream. Backgrounds: an *impetuous youth* first out
the gate, one *driven* by a wrong to set right, or one *destined* under
signs and portents. Signature work is heart: throwing themselves in over
their head and growing mid-fall, converting failure into growth faster than
any other playbook — misses feed both XP and the moves themselves. Plays
as the campaign's stakes made visible: the future the village is fighting
for.
