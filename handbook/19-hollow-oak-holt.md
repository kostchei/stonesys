# 19. The Hollow-Oak Holt

## The premise

Deep in an old forest, sheltered in a fold of hills, a small tribe of elves
lives in and around a single enormous, ancient hollow oak — dens and
platforms grown and woven into the great tree, wolf-trails threading out to
the hunting grounds, smoke curling from the Mother Tree's heart. The world
is hardening: each winter runs longer, humans push at the wood's edge, and
the game grows thin. Of late the tribe has taken in an outsider or two.

The magic here is nobody's loan. It is in the blood and the land — shapers
who coax living wood and stone, healers who mend flesh with their hands,
senders who speak mind to mind — and each generation shapes a little less,
remembers a little less, while the cold presses a little closer.

## Your home

The steading is the **Holt** (Scale 0, Safety +1, Prosperity 0, Influence
−1, Supply +1), with two facilities: the **Mother Tree**, shelter and heart
of the tribe, which hides and warms everyone through the worst weather but
cannot forgive fire; and the **Shaping-Grove**, where the shapers work wood,
stone, and flesh — the tribe's craft, healing, and quiet magic, resting on
a very few gifted hands.

The Holt's strange resource is the **old magic in the land** itself: it
cannot be mined or hoarded, only lived with and tended — and it draws the
notice of humans (as devilry) and of older things that remember when the
magic was stronger.

## Embedment style

**Village.** No patron, god, or bastion tracks — no pips at all on the
embedment rail. The bonds *are* the embedment: recognition, sending, pack
and tribe all live in the fill-in statements between characters and in each
playbook's community tie to the Holt. Chapters 12–14 are not used in this
campaign; the steading rules (chapter 15) very much are.

#### At the Forge  ·  *Signature*

> When you forge, mend, or improve arms and gear at a hot fire.

- **Failure:** It won't come right — bad metal, a hidden flaw, or the work costs more than you have.
- **▲ spend:** Hold 1 Craftwork (spend: a masterwork edge — +1 damage or a boost die with it / a hidden feature built in / mend it fast, right now / enough to arm several of the tribe). / You learn something true about the metal, or about who made a blade before you.
- **▼ costs:** It needs rare materials, or more time than you'll easily find. / The fire, the noise, or the smoke draws unwanted eyes.

- **✶ Despair:** The work fails and wastes precious metal, or the forge-fire brings danger down on the Holt.

**Hold — Craftwork.** Spend 1 to: A masterwork edge: +1 damage, or a boost die when the piece is used. / A hidden feature — a concealed blade, a catch, a mark only you know. / Mend it fast, right now, in the middle of trouble. / Turn out enough to arm several of the tribe at once.

#### Troll-Made  ·  *Fixed*

> Because trolls make the finest weapons, and you are a troll among elves:

Your work is prized and famous; a blade of your making opens doors and earns respect. But your outsider-ness is never forgotten — some elves will always see the troll first and the kin second, and other trolls may name you a traitor for making arms for elves.

#### Troll-Strong  ·  *Choice*

**Roll +STR** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Failure:** Even you meet something that won't give, and it costs you.
- **▲ spend:** Do it and keep something in reserve. / Your show of force makes lesser things think about fleeing.
- **▼ costs:** You strain something, or break what you meant to move. / The effort leaves you open.
- **◆ Triumph:** You do what no elf could, and they will not forget it.
- **✶ Despair:** You overreach, and the failure is as big as you are.

#### Hard to Hurt  ·  *Choice*

**Roll +CON** · default difficulty **average**.

- **▲ spend:** Shield a smaller ally behind your bulk. / Set a wall the tribe can rally to.
- **▼ costs:** You take it now and stiffen up for it later.
- **◆ Triumph:** You wade through what should have stopped you cold.
- **✶ Despair:** Something gets through even you, and badly.

#### Know Your Metal  ·  *Choice*

> When you examine a made thing — a blade, a lock, a mechanism, a hoard.

**Roll +INT** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** Ask the GM one: who made this and how well? what is it worth? where is it weak?

- **▲ spend:** Ask another. / You know how to break it, or improve it.
- **▼ costs:** Handling it, you triggered or marked it.
- **◆ Triumph:** You read the whole history and secret of the thing.
- **✶ Despair:** You misjudge a made thing badly, and stake something on it.

### Desert Elf

#### Speak for the Tribe  ·  *Choice*

### Rockshaper

*Stone answers you as wood answers the tree-shaper — it flows under your hands like clay, then sets hard as a mountain's root. You raise walls where the tribe needs shelter, open ways through the living rock, and feel the deep bones of the earth. It is slow, deep, patient magic, and it has made you a little like the stone itself.*

**Duty:** The stone is patient and it does not forget; shape it with respect, for what you raise will outlast you, and what you break may not be mended.

#### The Deep Bones  ·  *Fixed*

> Because you feel the stone and earth around you always:

- **✶ Despair:** A collapse — on you, on the way home, or on what you were digging toward.
