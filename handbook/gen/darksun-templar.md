### The Templar

*You wore a sorcerer-king's authority like a second skin — law, granted spells, and the power to ruin a life with a signature. You fled the city with the Legion, but the king's mark is still on you, and his power still answers when you call. That should frighten you more than it does.*

**Duty:** The authority was never yours. It was lent, and everything lent can be recalled — with interest.

Default array shown; you may swap up to 2 pairs. Anchor: **CHA 3+** — no swap may take it lower.

**HP** 16+CON · **Damage** d6 · **Load** 6+STR

**Names:** Vashti, Corun, Ith, Malla, Serd, Oyan, Bex, Tulla, Narek, Sef, Draya, Kol

**Look** — choose one per line:

- *Bearing:* commanding, coldly polite, watchful, used to being obeyed
- *Dress:* templar's robes, defiantly worn, the same robes, hidden away, authority in plain clothes, a single badge of office
- *Eyes:* hard, calculating, tired of power, hungry for it still
- *Mark:* the king's sigil branded or tattooed, a ring of office you cannot remove, a scar where a sigil was cut away, nothing; you hid it well

**Gear** — choose as marked:

- *Symbol:* a rod of office (d6) · sealed writs and a signet · a ceremonial blade (d6)
- *Cover:* fine city clothes · a bureaucrat's document case · a disguise as a common refugee
- *Secret:* a list of the king's other agents · blackmail on a rival templar · the real reason you fled

#### Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

#### By the King's Word  ·  *Signature*

> When you invoke your borrowed authority to command, cow, or requisition.

**Roll +CHA** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** They obey, for now.
- **Failure:** They refuse, and worse, they wonder why a templar is out here at all.
- **▲ spend:** Hold 1 Writ (spend: they obey without question / lesser officials or guards fall in to help / a gate, door, or record opens to you / you learn who truly holds power here). / You read exactly how far your authority will carry in this place.
- **▼ costs:** Someone tests your authority instead of bowing to it. / Word travels toward the city that a runaway templar was seen.
- **◆ Triumph:** Total compliance, and someone useful decides to attach themselves to you.
- **✶ Despair:** They know you are a renegade, and they act on it now.

**Hold — Writ.** Spend 1 to: They obey without question or delay. / Lesser officials, guards, or clerks fall in to help you. / A gate, a door, a strongbox, or a record opens to you. / You learn who truly holds power in this place.

#### Granted Spells  ·  *Fixed*

> When you demand power from the sorcerer-king who marked you (Invoke Patron — chapter 12):

The king's magic answers — but each use adds 1 Debt to him and risks him sensing where you are. This is the dangerous tie: the more you lean on his power, the more surely he finds you.

#### The Letter of the Law  ·  *Choice*

> When you reach for a law, a right, a precedent, or a loophole.

**Roll +INT** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You know a statute or custom that serves you here, and how to wield it.
- **Failure:** The law here is not what you thought, or does not run the way you need.
- **▲ spend:** You find a second angle in the law. / You cite it with such certainty no one dares check.
- **▼ costs:** Someone here knows the law better than you. / Invoking it draws official attention.
- **◆ Triumph:** You find the exact clause that makes them yours.
- **✶ Despair:** You cite a law that condemns you.

#### Interrogate  ·  *Choice*

> When you break a subject down to get the truth from them.

**Roll +CHA** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** They give you something true and useful.
- **Failure:** They give you nothing, or a lie you swallow.
- **▲ spend:** They keep talking, past what they meant to say. / They will fear you enough to come to you next time.
- **▼ costs:** You break more than you meant to. / They will hate you, and remember.
- **◆ Triumph:** They tell you everything, and thank you for the mercy of stopping.
- **✶ Despair:** You've tortured a loyal person, and the Legion sees what you are.

#### Smite the Defiant  ·  *Choice*

> When you spend the king's granted power to blast, curse, or bind an enemy:

**Roll +CHA** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** The power strikes as you command.
- **Failure:** It sputters or misfires, and everyone sees a templar's magic fail.
- **▲ spend:** Strike or bind more than one. / The display cows everyone watching.
- **▼ costs:** Add 1 Debt to the king as you draw hard on his power. / The magic flares where it will be reported.
- **◆ Triumph:** A show of power that ends the confrontation outright.
- **✶ Despair:** The king feels you use his gift, and turns his attention this way.

#### Court Sense  ·  *Choice*

> When you study any gathering where power is being brokered:

Take a boost die and ask the GM who here truly outranks whom, regardless of what titles say. You have spent a life reading exactly this.

#### Embedment

*patron*

Start: Bond 1, Debt 3, Heat 1.

**Bonds** — fill in at least two:

- ____ knew me when I still served the king, and has not decided if I've changed.
- ____ suffered under a writ I signed, and I owe them more than an apology.
- ____ believes I can protect the Legion with what I know of the city; I fear I will only draw the city down on us.
- I hold a secret that would destroy ____, and I have not decided whether I am the kind of person who uses it.
- ____ is the only one who calls me by my name and not my old office.

**Community tie** — choose one:

- The Free Legion needs someone who understands the city's power; ____ vouches for you against those who'd string up any templar.
- You could sell the Legion back to the king for your own pardon; ____ is the reason you never quite do it.
- You use scraps of your old authority to shield the Legion; ____ knows how thin that bluff really is.

**Tracks:**

- **Bond** (0–3, start 1). Standing with the sorcerer-king. Low is safer, here.
- **Debt** (0–6, start 3). At 6 the king makes a major demand, then resets to 3. He always finds a way to collect.
- **Heat** (0–4, start 1). How close the city's hunt has come.

#### Advancement

**Basic** (5 XP each):

- ☐ Take another Templar move.
- ☐ Take another Templar move.
- ☐ Take a move from another playbook.
- ☐ Take a move from another playbook.
- ☐ Increase your maximum held Writ by 1.
- ☐ Forge credentials: name an office or authority you can convincingly claim anywhere.
- ☐ Train a move you lean on: it upgrades 2 dice to proficiency (never more than its stat).
- ☐ Cut a thread to the king: lower your standing Debt by 1, permanently, at some cost.

**Advanced:**

- ☐ Raise one stat by 1 (max 5). Rare, and only at a true turning point.
- ☐ Sever the sigil: end the king's hold for good, resolving the fallout in play, and lose the granted spells that came with it.
- ☐ Claim a jurisdiction: found a Bastion (a hall of law) where your authority is real because you make it so.
