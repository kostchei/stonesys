### Master Hunter

*You feed the tribe and guard its edges, running the wild with a bond-wolf at your side and a bow that does not miss. The hunt is not sport to you; it is life and death for every mouth in the Holt. You know the wood the way you know your own heartbeat, and the wolf that runs with you knows your soul.*

**Duty:** The tribe eats because you hunt, and sleeps because you watch. The wild is your charge, and your bond-wolf your other half.

#### Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

#### The Hunt  ·  *Signature*

> When you stalk and strike your quarry — beast or foe — from cover and at the right moment.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** Your shot or strike lands, and you are still unseen.
- **Failure:** The quarry breaks, or the strike goes wide, and now it knows you're there.
- **▲ spend:** Hold 1 Quarry (spend: a killing shot, clean and quick / stay hidden even after the strike / drive the quarry where you want it / bring down enough to feed the tribe well). / You read the quarry — where it will run, what it guards, what it fears.
- **▼ costs:** You loose your last good arrows, or your position is given away. / The hunt runs long and hard, drawing you far from the tribe or into worse country.
- **◆ Triumph:** A clean kill that will be a song at the fire, and meat for many days.
- **✶ Despair:** The hunted turns hunter, and now it is stalking you.

**Hold — Quarry.** Spend 1 to: A killing shot or strike, clean and quick. / Stay hidden even after you strike. / Drive the quarry exactly where you want it. / Bring down enough to feed the tribe well.

#### Wolf-Friend  ·  *Fixed*

> Because a bond-wolf runs at your side, soul to soul:

Your wolf hunts, guards, and fights with you as one; take a boost die when you act together, and it will do things for you no wild beast would. But the bond runs both ways: your wolf is not a tool but kin, and its death would wound your very soul. Where you go, you weigh whether the wolf should follow.

#### Track  ·  *Choice*

> When you read trail, spoor, and sign to follow something, or to know what passed.

**Roll +WIS** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** The trail speaks: you know what passed, when, and where it went.
- **Failure:** The sign is cold or crossed, and following it wrong will cost you.
- **▲ spend:** Learn the number, health, or mood of what you track. / Find where they can be caught, or avoided.
- **▼ costs:** Tracking them, you left a trail of your own. / The trail leads somewhere dangerous.
- **◆ Triumph:** You could follow them to the ends of the wood.
- **✶ Despair:** The trail was laid for you to follow.

#### Bowmaster  ·  *Choice*

> When you take a shot that only a master could make.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** The arrow flies true and does exactly what you needed.
- **Failure:** The shot fails, and you've spent it and shown your hand.
- **▲ spend:** Keep the range: they can't close before you loose again. / The shot does more — disarms, pins, drops a leader.
- **▼ costs:** Your last good arrow is gone. / The shot gives away your position.
- **◆ Triumph:** An impossible shot that decides the moment.
- **✶ Despair:** You miss the one shot that mattered.

#### Run with the Pack  ·  *Choice*

> When you pursue, flee, or range far across the wild, alone or with wolf and hunters.

**Roll +CON** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You cover the ground, tireless, and get where you must be.
- **Failure:** The wild wears you down, or turns you from your path.
- **▲ spend:** Bring the whole hunting party along at your pace. / Arrive unseen, or fresh enough to fight.
- **▼ costs:** The running costs you: a setback until you rest. / You range so far you're cut off from help.
- **◆ Triumph:** You run down what fled, or outrun what chased, without fail.
- **✶ Despair:** You outrun your own strength, or your pack, at the worst moment.

- ☐ Run with a pack: your bond-wolf brings its kin, and you can call on wolves at need.
- ☐ Claim the hunting grounds: found a Bastion (a hunt-lodge and secured range) that feeds the tribe through any winter.
