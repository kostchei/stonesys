### The Sanctioned Thief

*You steal with a license. The Thieves' Guild takes its cut and, in return, no one is supposed to slit your throat for working their city. It is a good arrangement, so long as you never forget who truly owns your hands.*

**Duty:** The Guild grants the right to steal and can revoke it with a word. Unsanctioned, you are just meat the city has not finished with yet.

#### Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

#### The Quiet Job  ·  *Signature*

> When you break in, lift a prize, and get out without raising the house.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You get the prize and get clear.
- **Failure:** You get one but not the other — caught with the goods, or clear but empty-handed.
- **▲ spend:** Hold 1 Score (spend: you also grabbed something extra and valuable / you left no trace at all / you know a second way out / you spotted the trap before it sprung). / You learn the house's real secret — who they fear, what else they hide.
- **▼ costs:** You leave a thread that leads back to the Black Cat. / Add 1 Heat: a witness, an alarm, or a name spoken.
- **◆ Triumph:** A perfect job — they won't even know they were robbed until it's far too late.
- **✶ Despair:** The job was bait, and the jaws are closing now.

**Hold — Score.** Spend 1 to: You grabbed something extra and valuable on the way. / You left no trace; no one knows a theft occurred. / You have a second, unwatched way out. / You spotted and beat a trap or guard before it mattered.

#### Sanctioned  ·  *Fixed*

> When you lean on your Guild standing for a fence, a safe rooftop, a name, or a blind eye (Invoke Patron — chapter 12):

The Guild provides, and adds 1 Debt. Steal without sanction, cheat the Guild of its cut, or embarrass it, and you risk being declared unsanctioned — after which every knife in the city is licensed against you.

#### Cat-Foot  ·  *Choice*

> When you climb, balance, tumble, or move without a sound.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You go where you please, quiet and unseen.
- **Failure:** You slip, or you're spotted at the worst moment.
- **▲ spend:** Bring another along, just as quiet. / You end up perfectly placed for what's next.
- **▼ costs:** You make it, but leave a mark or a sound behind you. / You're committed; there's no going back the way you came.
- **◆ Triumph:** You move like the fog itself; no one will believe you were there.
- **✶ Despair:** You're seen by exactly the wrong pair of eyes.

#### Sleight  ·  *Choice*

> When you pick a pocket or a lock, palm a thing, or plant one on someone.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** It's done, clean and unseen.
- **Failure:** A hand closes on your wrist, or the lock defeats you noisily.
- **▲ spend:** Grab a little extra while you're at it. / They'll never suspect it was you.
- **▼ costs:** You leave a trace that points back. / It takes longer than you have.
- **◆ Triumph:** You do it under their nose and they smile at you.
- **✶ Despair:** You're caught red-handed by someone who matters.

#### Case the Mark  ·  *Choice*

> When you study a place or a person you mean to rob.

**Roll +INT** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** Ask the GM one: where's the prize? what guards it? when is it weakest?
- **Failure:** You misjudge it, and the mistake waits for you inside.
- **▲ spend:** Ask another. / You find a way in no one else would think of.
- **▼ costs:** Someone noticed you casing the place. / Your read is already going stale.
- **◆ Triumph:** You know the job cold, every guard and every gap.
- **✶ Despair:** While you cased them, they cased you.

#### Guild Ties  ·  *Choice*

> When you need a fence, a forger, a lookout, or a name in a hurry:

Name a Guild contact you can reach here and take a boost die dealing with them. The GM says what small price or gossip they attach to the favor.
