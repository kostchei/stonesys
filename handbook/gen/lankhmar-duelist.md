### The Back-Alley Duelist

*You are the best blade in a bad quarter, and everyone knows it — which is exactly the problem. Your reputation feeds you, shields the band, and draws every bravo who wants to be the one who put you down. You have turned a few square feet of foggy lane into a place men do not draw on your friends.*

**Duty:** Your name is the band's wall. A wall is only as good as the last man who tested it and fell.

**Look** — choose one per line:

- *Bearing:* insolent grace, cold economy, showy flourish, watchful stillness
- *Dress:* threadbare finery, practical and dark, a duelist's slashed sleeves, whatever's clean enough to be seen in
- *Blade:* a slender rapier, a saber with a story, sword and long dagger both, a plain, wickedly kept blade
- *Mark:* a famous scar, a duelist's guild-token you have no right to, rings from those you've bested, a reputation and nothing to show for it

**Gear** — choose as marked:

- *Blade:* a fine rapier (d8) · saber and main-gauche (d8) · a plain sword kept razor-true (d8)
- *Guard:* a swirling cloak · a buckler · quickness and nerve, nothing more
- *Token:* a rival's blade you took · a duelist's code you actually keep · a debt of honor owed to you

#### Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

#### Cross Blades  ·  *Signature*

> When you fight one or a few with the blade, up close and personal.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You have the better of them; press and they fall or flee.
- **Failure:** The exchange goes against you; the GM says how it costs you.
- **▲ spend:** Hold 1 Flourish (spend: disarm or wound without killing / drive a foe back and seize ground / turn a blow meant for an ally / cow the onlookers with your skill). / You read your opponent's style and its one weakness.
- **▼ costs:** You take a hit, or your guard is left open. / The fight draws a crowd, the watch, or a name you'd rather not fight.
- **◆ Triumph:** A single perfect stroke ends it, and the tale of it will spread.
- **✶ Despair:** You are badly wounded, or you kill someone you should not have.

**Hold — Flourish.** Spend 1 to: Disarm or wound your foe instead of killing them. / Drive a foe back and take the ground or the doorway. / Turn aside a blow meant for someone beside you. / Cow the onlookers with a display of pure skill.

#### This Is Our Ground  ·  *Fixed*

> On the band's own turf around the Black Cat, where your name is law:

Lead the band's defense of its turf well and raise your Standing; let the turf be taken or your name be humbled and lower it. At high Standing, most bravos will not draw on you or yours here at all — your reputation does the fighting for you.

#### Duelist's Eye  ·  *Choice*

> When you size up a fighter or a fight before steel is drawn.

**Roll +WIS** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** Ask the GM one: how good are they, truly? what are they hiding? what will they do first? Acting on it grants a boost die.
- **Failure:** You misread them, and it shows once blades are out.
- **▲ spend:** Ask another. / You choose the ground the fight happens on.
- **▼ costs:** They saw you measuring them, and take offense.
- **◆ Triumph:** You know exactly how to beat them before you begin.
- **✶ Despair:** Your read is their bait.

#### Riposte  ·  *Choice*

> When you turn an enemy's attack and answer it in the same breath.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** Their blow slides off and yours goes home.
- **Failure:** You misjudge the timing and take the hit instead.
- **▲ spend:** Leave them open for an ally. / Take their weapon or their balance.
- **▼ costs:** Your counter commits you; you can't shift to a new foe.
- **◆ Triumph:** You turn their own attack into their undoing.
- **✶ Despair:** The feint was the real attack, and you fell for it.

#### Swagger  ·  *Choice*

> When you trade on your fearsome name to warn off, provoke, or awe someone:

Take a boost die to make them back down, lose their nerve, or fight you on your terms. Naming a duel you won that they witnessed grants a second boost die — but a reputation is a debt the whole city may one day call in.

#### Second's Honor  ·  *Choice*

> When you stand as a shield for someone who cannot fight for themselves.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** No blade reaches them while you stand.
- **Failure:** You are pressed too hard, or holding the line costs you dearly.
- **▲ spend:** Hold 1 Flourish. / Draw the attackers' whole attention onto you.
- **▼ costs:** You hold, but you're pinned and cannot leave them to help elsewhere.
- **◆ Triumph:** You make defending them look so costly the attackers give up.
- **✶ Despair:** You hold, and it is nearly the end of you.

#### Embedment

*bastion*

Start: Standing 1.

**Bonds** — fill in at least two:

- ____ has stood at my back in a fight and not run; that is rarer than gold.
- ____ thinks my dueling will get us all killed one day, and may be right.
- I am teaching ____ the blade, and fear the reputation it will hang on them.
- ____ once saw me lose a fight I should have won, and has never mentioned it.
- I owe ____ a life; they pulled me out of a duel I could not win.

**Community tie** — choose one:

- The Black Cat sleeps easier because bravos fear your name; ____ knows how thin that reputation wears.
- When trouble comes to the turf, ____ comes to fetch you first.
- You keep the quarter's peace with your blade, but ____ remembers the ones you've killed to do it.

**Tracks:**

- **Standing** (0–3, start 1). Your name's weight on the band's turf; how much the quarter defers to you.

#### Advancement

**Basic** (5 XP each):

- ☐ Take another Duelist move.
- ☐ Take another Duelist move.
- ☐ Take a move from another playbook.
- ☐ Take a move from another playbook.
- ☐ Increase your maximum held Flourish by 1.
- ☐ Grow your legend: raise your Standing by 1 through a duel the whole quarter will retell.
- ☐ Train a move you lean on: it upgrades 2 dice to proficiency (never more than its stat).
- ☐ Master a signature stroke that is yours and no one else's.

**Advanced:**

- ☐ Raise one stat by 1 (max 5). Rare, and only at a true turning point.
- ☐ Retire the blade: hang up your reputation and become someone new, resolving the fallout in play.
- ☐ Open a school: found a Bastion (a fencing hall) where your name draws students and shields the band.
