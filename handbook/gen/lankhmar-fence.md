### The Antiquarian Fence

*You buy what fell off a cart and sell it to people who ask no questions, and along the way you have learned more about this city's forgotten history than any scholar — because the past keeps turning up stolen. You know what old things are worth, and what they will do, and which of them should never have been dug up at all.*

**Duty:** Every treasure has a buyer, and every buyer has a leash. Your web of collectors keeps you rich and keeps you owned; the trick is selling the relic without becoming one.

#### Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

#### What It's Worth  ·  *Signature*

> When you appraise a thing and set about turning it into coin or leverage.

**Roll +CHA** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You know its true worth and find a buyer or a use for it.
- **Failure:** You misprice it, or the only buyer is one you'd rather not deal with.
- **▲ spend:** Hold 1 Connections (spend: name a collector here who wants exactly this / a buyer who owes you a favor / a discreet appraiser or forger on call / a rumor of where more of it can be found). / You know the thing's history — and its secret, dangerous or profitable.
- **▼ costs:** The sale leaves a thread the Guild or the law can follow. / The buyer wants more than coin — a favor, a silence, a further job.
- **◆ Triumph:** You find the one buyer who will pay a fortune and ask nothing.
- **✶ Despair:** The piece is hotter than you knew, and its true owner is coming for it, and you.

**Hold — Connections.** Spend 1 to: Name a collector here who wants exactly this. / Call in a buyer who already owes you a favor. / Produce a discreet appraiser, forger, or go-between on short notice. / Know a rumor of where more of this kind of thing can be found.

#### The Collectors' Web  ·  *Fixed*

> When you draw on your network of collectors, forgers, and black-market buyers (Invoke Patron — chapter 12):

The web provides — credit, discretion, a market for anything — and adds 1 Debt. Your patrons are wealthy, patient, and possessive; disappoint a collector, or sell them a fake they detect, and the debt is collected in ways coin cannot cover.

#### Old Lore  ·  *Choice*

> When you dig into the history, provenance, or hidden nature of an old or arcane thing.

**Roll +INT** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** Ask the GM one: what is it, truly? what does it do? who made it, and who wants it now?
- **Failure:** The lore is tangled or false, and acting on it will mislead you.
- **▲ spend:** Ask another. / You learn how to use it, or how to make it safe.
- **▼ costs:** Your questions reach the wrong scholarly ear. / Knowing what it is, you now cannot un-know the danger.
- **◆ Triumph:** You understand it completely, worth and peril alike.
- **✶ Despair:** You've learned the thing's secret, and it has learned you know.

#### Drive the Bargain  ·  *Choice*

> When you haggle, flatter, or maneuver toward the deal you want.

**Roll +CHA** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You close a deal you can live with.
- **Failure:** No deal, or one whose price you'll come to regret.
- **▲ spend:** A better price or terms. / A favor or morsel of news thrown in. / A standing arrangement for the future.
- **▼ costs:** You've slighted someone with reach. / Word of the deal travels.
- **◆ Triumph:** You get everything and their gratitude besides.
- **✶ Despair:** The deal was the trap, and you've stepped in it.

#### Spot the Fake  ·  *Choice*

> When you examine a thing, a document, or a story for whether it's genuine.

**Roll +INT** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You know true from false, and how you know.
- **Failure:** You're taken in, or you cry fake on the genuine article.
- **▲ spend:** You spot who made the fake and why. / You could make a better one yourself.
- **▼ costs:** The forger learns you're onto them.
- **◆ Triumph:** You see every seam; nothing gets past you.
- **✶ Despair:** You stake something real on a judgment that's dead wrong.

#### Discreet Word  ·  *Choice*

> When you need a name, a rumor, or a quiet introduction in the world of buyers and thieves:

Take a boost die to reach the right person through your web. The GM says what small price, gossip, or obligation rides along with the favor.
