### The Poison-Bard

*You sing, you charm, you kill. In this land a bard is an entertainer, a spy, and a poisoner-for-hire, all in one bright costume — and the guild that trained you takes its cut of every performance, especially the fatal ones.*

**Duty:** The best murders look like applause. The guild taught you that, and the guild never stops being owed.

**Look** — choose one per line:

- *Bearing:* dazzling, disarming, watchful behind a smile, quietly amused
- *Dress:* performer's bright motley, elegant and forgettable, practical with one flourish, a costume for every crowd
- *Eyes:* laughing, measuring you for something, warm and false, tired of the act
- *Tell:* rings that are not just rings, a voice that carries anywhere, quick, careful hands, you always know where the exits are

**Gear** — choose as marked:

- *Instrument:* a hollow-necked lute (hides much) · a singer's voice and a hidden blade (d6) · a set of pipes and a needle
- *Craft:* a poisoner's kit · disguises and cosmetics · a folio of songs, codes, and secrets
- *Guild-mark:* a contract token · a debt-tally the guild keeps on you · a name that opens certain doors

#### Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

#### Hold the Room  ·  *Signature*

> When you perform to sway a crowd, hold their eyes, or work a gathering.

**Roll +CHA** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** They are rapt. For as long as you hold them, the room is yours.
- **Failure:** You lose them, or the wrong person sees through the act.
- **▲ spend:** Hold 1 Performance (spend: slip away or into somewhere unseen / plant a rumor or a coded word / lift a purse, a key, or a letter under cover of applause / doctor a cup or a dish unremarked). / You read the room's true mood and who really rules it.
- **▼ costs:** A heckler, a rival, or a suspicious eye disrupts the act. / Your message reaches an ear it was never meant for.
- **◆ Triumph:** The whole room moves as you want it to.
- **✶ Despair:** Someone in the crowd was performing back, and they are better than you.

**Hold — Performance.** Spend 1 to: Slip away, or into somewhere you shouldn't be, unseen. / Plant a rumor, a coded word, or a suggestion. / Lift a purse, a key, or a letter under cover of the crowd. / Doctor a cup, a dish, or a blade without anyone marking it.

#### Guild Cut  ·  *Fixed*

> When you call on the guild for a contract, a poison, a safehouse, or a name (Invoke Patron — chapter 12):

The guild provides, and adds 1 Debt. It collects in coin, in silence, or in blood, and it decides which. No one leaves the guild owing.

#### Poisoner's Craft  ·  *Choice*

> When you brew, choose, or apply a poison, or work out what one has done.

**Roll +INT** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You have the right poison for the need, and know how to deliver it.
- **Failure:** You lack what you need, or misjudge the dose or the victim.
- **▲ spend:** It is subtle: slow, deniable, or mistakable for illness. / You have the antidote, too.
- **▼ costs:** It leaves a trace a knowing eye could find. / You handle it carelessly and catch a touch yourself.
- **◆ Triumph:** Undetectable, and perfectly timed.
- **✶ Despair:** It works on the wrong person, or is traced straight back to you.

#### Silver Tongue  ·  *Choice*

> When you talk someone into what you want with charm, flattery, or a beautiful lie.

**Roll +CHA** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** They come around to your way, at least for now.
- **Failure:** They don't buy it, and now they are wary of you.
- **▲ spend:** They do you an extra kindness. / They will not remember it was your idea.
- **▼ costs:** The lie has a short life; the GM says when it comes due. / Someone else saw through it.
- **◆ Triumph:** They become your willing ally.
- **✶ Despair:** Your charm lands you a devoted enemy.

#### Read the Mark  ·  *Choice*

> When you study a person you mean to charm, rob, or kill.

**Roll +WIS** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** Ask the GM one: what do they crave? who do they trust? when are they alone and unguarded?
- **Failure:** You misread them, and it will show at the worst moment.
- **▲ spend:** Ask another. / You learn their one real weakness.
- **▼ costs:** They notice your interest. / Getting close enough to learn this exposed you.
- **◆ Triumph:** You know them well enough to become whoever they most want to see.
- **✶ Despair:** The mark was studying you the whole time.

#### Killing Grace  ·  *Choice*

> When you strike a sudden, precise blow with blade, needle, or worse, on someone who does not expect it.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** The blow lands true, and quietly.
- **Failure:** You miss the moment, and now they know what you are.
- **▲ spend:** No one else realizes what happened, yet. / You are already moving away.
- **▼ costs:** It is messier or louder than you wanted. / A witness.
- **◆ Triumph:** Clean, silent, and unseen.
- **✶ Despair:** The blow lands and does not stop them, and now they are looking right at you.

#### Embedment

*patron*

Start: Bond 1, Debt 2, Heat 0.

**Bonds** — fill in at least two:

- ____ has been my audience and my alibi more than once.
- The guild once had me mark ____ for death, and I did not do it. I have not told them why.
- ____ knows a song I sang for a killing, and could hang me with it.
- ____ believes the performer and not the poisoner, and I let them.
- I owe ____ for a secret they kept about a job gone wrong.

**Community tie** — choose one:

- The Free Legion loves the songs and doesn't ask about the rest; ____ suspects what your bright costume hides.
- The guild wants eyes and a blade inside the Legion; ____ is the one you refuse to report on.
- You keep the Legion's spirits up when nothing else can; ____ knows how much that performance costs you.

**Tracks:**

- **Bond** (0–3, start 1). Standing with your performers' guild.
- **Debt** (0–6, start 2). At 6 the guild makes a major demand, then resets to 3.
- **Heat** (0–4, start 0). Attention from marks, rivals, and the watch.

- ☐ Build a troupe: found a Bastion (a playhouse and its network of eyes) for the Legion.
