### Troll Weaponsmith

*You are a troll, and trolls make the finest weapons in the world — everyone knows it, which is why the elves let you stay even though half of them will never trust a troll. You came to the Hollow-Oak as an outsider and, slowly, became something like kin. Your hammer keeps the tribe armed against a world that is closing in.*

**Duty:** Your craft is why they keep you, and never quite why they trust you. Every blade you forge is a bond you're building, one strike at a time.

**HP** 20+CON · **Damage** d8 · **Load** 8+STR

**Names:** Grethka, Bolg, Ironjaw, Muddle, Krung, Sootgrim, Varra, Hodd, Ashk, Dwerric, Gnorl, Torbek

**Look** — choose one per line:

- *Build:* squat and boulder-strong, gnarled and enormous, broad, scarred, and sooty, surprisingly deft for all the bulk
- *Bearing:* gruff, watchful of slights, dryly patient, prouder of the work than of anything
- *Craft-mark:* burn-scarred forearms, a maker's brand on every blade, hands that are half tool, an apron of ancient leather
- *Eyes:* shrewd, banked like coals, quick to judge quality, softer than the face lets on

**Gear** — choose as marked:

- *Tool-weapon:* a great smith's hammer (d8) · a cleaver of your own making (d8) · tongs and a heated bar (d6)
- *Forge:* a portable smithy and bellows · a hoard of good metal · rare troll-lore of metals
- *Keepsake:* the first blade you ever made · a debt of iron owed by another troll · a token from the elf who first spoke for you

#### Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

#### At the Forge  ·  *Signature*

> When you forge, mend, or improve arms and gear at a hot fire.

**Roll +STR** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You make or mend the thing the tribe needs, and it is good work.
- **Failure:** It won't come right — bad metal, a hidden flaw, or the work costs more than you have.
- **▲ spend:** Hold 1 Craftwork (spend: a masterwork edge — +1 damage or a boost die with it / a hidden feature built in / mend it fast, right now / enough to arm several of the tribe). / You learn something true about the metal, or about who made a blade before you.
- **▼ costs:** It needs rare materials, or more time than you'll easily find. / The fire, the noise, or the smoke draws unwanted eyes.
- **◆ Triumph:** A masterwork that will outlive you and become a story the tribe tells.
- **✶ Despair:** The work fails and wastes precious metal, or the forge-fire brings danger down on the Holt.

**Hold — Craftwork.** Spend 1 to: A masterwork edge: +1 damage, or a boost die when the piece is used. / A hidden feature — a concealed blade, a catch, a mark only you know. / Mend it fast, right now, in the middle of trouble. / Turn out enough to arm several of the tribe at once.

#### Troll-Made  ·  *Fixed*

> Because trolls make the finest weapons, and you are a troll among elves:

Your work is prized and famous; a blade of your making opens doors and earns respect. But your outsider-ness is never forgotten — some elves will always see the troll first and the kin second, and other trolls may name you a traitor for making arms for elves.

#### Troll-Strong  ·  *Choice*

> When you put your massive strength to a task — a fight, a burden, a barred way.

**Roll +STR** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** Sheer strength carries the moment: the foe reels, the weight moves, the way opens.
- **Failure:** Even you meet something that won't give, and it costs you.
- **▲ spend:** Do it and keep something in reserve. / Your show of force makes lesser things think about fleeing.
- **▼ costs:** You strain something, or break what you meant to move. / The effort leaves you open.
- **◆ Triumph:** You do what no elf could, and they will not forget it.
- **✶ Despair:** You overreach, and the failure is as big as you are.

#### Hard to Hurt  ·  *Choice*

> When blows, cold, or hardship that would fell an elf come down on your troll-tough hide.

**Roll +CON** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You shrug it off and keep standing.
- **Failure:** Even troll-hide has its limit, and you've found it.
- **▲ spend:** Shield a smaller ally behind your bulk. / Set a wall the tribe can rally to.
- **▼ costs:** You take it now and stiffen up for it later.
- **◆ Triumph:** You wade through what should have stopped you cold.
- **✶ Despair:** Something gets through even you, and badly.

#### Know Your Metal  ·  *Choice*

> When you examine a made thing — a blade, a lock, a mechanism, a hoard.

**Roll +INT** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** Ask the GM one: who made this and how well? what is it worth? where is it weak?
- **Failure:** It fools even your eye, and that costs you.
- **▲ spend:** Ask another. / You know how to break it, or improve it.
- **▼ costs:** Handling it, you triggered or marked it.
- **◆ Triumph:** You read the whole history and secret of the thing.
- **✶ Despair:** You misjudge a made thing badly, and stake something on it.

#### Embedment


**Bonds** — fill in at least two:

- ____ was the first elf to speak for me, and I would forge a blade for their hand before any other.
- ____ still watches me like the troll I am, and I am slowly, stubbornly proving them wrong.
- I am teaching ____ the craft, though trolls are not meant to share it.
- ____ carries a blade of my making, and while they hold it I feel bound to keep them alive.
- I owe ____ for a kindness shown to an outsider who had earned none.

**Community tie** — choose one:

- The Hollow-Oak Holt keeps you for your hammer; ____ is the one who keeps you for yourself.
- When the tribe must fight, they fight with your work in their hands; ____ makes sure you know it matters.
- Some in the Holt would still send the troll away; ____ stands between you and them.

#### Advancement

**Basic** (5 XP each):

- ☐ Take another Troll Weaponsmith move.
- ☐ Take another Troll Weaponsmith move.
- ☐ Take a move from another playbook.
- ☐ Take a move from another playbook.
- ☐ Increase your maximum held Craftwork by 1.
- ☐ Learn a lost forging: name a kind of masterwork (troll-steel, a shaped fitting) only you can make.
- ☐ Train a move you lean on: it upgrades 2 dice to proficiency (never more than its stat).
- ☐ Win a doubter: turn one elf who distrusts the troll into a friend.

**Advanced:**

- ☐ Raise one stat by 1 (max 5). Rare, and only at a true turning point.
- ☐ Become truly kin: the tribe names you one of their own, troll or not.
- ☐ Raise a great forge: found a Bastion (a smithy) that makes the Holt a power others come to for arms.
