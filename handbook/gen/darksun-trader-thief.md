### The Trader-Thief

*You move the goods the houses cannot touch openly, and lift what isn't nailed down along the way. A house name at your back opens gates in every city. It also means a house owns a piece of you.*

**Duty:** Everything is for sale, including you, and the trick is never letting anyone learn your price.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 4 | 2 | 3 | 2 | 3 |

Default array shown; you may swap up to 2 pairs. Anchor: **DEX 3+** — no swap may take it lower.

**HP** 16+CON · **Damage** d6 · **Load** 6+STR

**Names:** Jesh, Corla, Nim, Balt, Sika, Renn, Ova, Dask, Lera, Fenn, Ptal, Wisa

**Look** — choose one per line:

- *Bearing:* easy and open, quick-eyed, unbothered, always leaving
- *Dress:* a house sash worn or hidden, travel-worn practical, one good piece and rags, whatever the crowd wears
- *Hands:* never still, ringed and ink-marked, callused and quick, kept in sight to seem honest
- *Eyes:* friendly, appraising, guarded, laughing

**Gear** — choose as marked:

- *Blade:* a hidden knife (d6) · a merchant's short sword (d6) · a weighted cane (d6)
- *Kit:* a smuggler's false-bottom pack · lockpicks and wax · a set of honest-looking scales
- *Stake:* a purse of house credit · a ledger of who owes whom · a single rare trade-good

#### Moves

You begin with the signature move, every fixed move, and 2 choice moves.

#### Sleight & Smuggle  ·  *Signature*

> When you move something past someone who would stop it — goods, a blade, or yourself.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** It gets through, unseen or unquestioned.
- **Failure:** You are caught in the act, or forced to abandon the thing to get clear.
- **▲ spend:** Hold 1 Angle (spend: a hidden pocket held just what you need / a guard here already owes you / you palmed something extra on the way / you have a clean exit ready). / You learn who really controls this gate, dock, or market.
- **▼ costs:** Add 1 Heat: someone marks your face. / It costs a bribe, a favor, or a piece of the goods to get clear.
- **◆ Triumph:** You move something huge, and no one will ever know it was you.
- **✶ Despair:** Caught red-handed by exactly the wrong person.

**Hold — Angles.** Spend 1 to: A hidden pocket or cache holds just what you need. / Someone nearby already owes you and will look the other way. / You palmed something useful on the way through. / You have a clean exit prepared.

#### House Name  ·  *Fixed*

> When you invoke your merchant house for goods, credit, passage, or muscle (Invoke Patron — chapter 12):

The house answers where it trades, and adds 1 Debt. The house always collects, and it does not forgive being embarrassed.

#### Appraise  ·  *Choice*

> When you weigh a person, a good, or a deal for what it is truly worth.

**Roll +INT** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You know its real value, and to whom it is worth the most.
- **Failure:** You misjudge it, and someone profits from your mistake.
- **▲ spend:** You spot the flaw, the fake, or the hidden owner. / You know exactly what price will move them.
- **▼ costs:** Your interest tips someone off that this is valuable.
- **◆ Triumph:** You see the whole web of who wants this and why.
- **✶ Despair:** You badly overvalue a thing, and stake something real on it.

#### Haggle  ·  *Choice*

> When you drive a bargain with someone who has what you want.

**Roll +CHA** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You reach a deal you can live with.
- **Failure:** There is no deal, or the price is one you will regret.
- **▲ spend:** A better price. / A favor or a piece of news thrown in. / A standing arrangement for next time.
- **▼ costs:** You've insulted someone with reach. / Word of the deal travels.
- **◆ Triumph:** You get everything you wanted and their goodwill besides.
- **✶ Despair:** The deal is a trap you just walked into.

#### Fast Hands  ·  *Choice*

> When you pick a lock or a pocket, slip a binding, or make something vanish.

**Roll +DEX** · default difficulty **average**.

- **Success:** You do it, clean and quick.
- **Failure:** You fumble it, and someone notices.
- **▲ spend:** No one saw a thing. / You get a second small thing while you're at it.
- **▼ costs:** You leave a trace that points back to you. / It takes longer than you have.
- **◆ Triumph:** You do it under their nose and they thank you for it.
- **✶ Despair:** A hand closes on your wrist.

#### Contacts  ·  *Choice*

> When you arrive somewhere the house has ever done business:

Name a contact you have here — a fence, a guide, a corrupt official, an old flame — and take a boost die dealing with them. The GM says what has changed since you last leaned on them.

#### Embedment

*patron*

Start: Bond 1, Debt 2, Heat 0.

**Bonds** — fill in at least two:

- ____ has been my partner on jobs the house would never sanction.
- I sold ____ out once to save my own skin, and they don't know it was me.
- ____ keeps my books honest, or honest enough, and I trust no one else with them.
- ____ thinks I can get the Legion the supplies it needs, and I've let them believe it.
- I owe ____ a debt I cannot pay in coin.

**Community tie** — choose one:

- You feed the Free Legion through channels only you can reach; ____ knows the house takes a cut.
- The Legion tolerates your thieving because it eats; ____ has warned you what happens the day it doesn't.
- Your house wants the Legion as a customer or a mark; ____ is the one you'd never let them touch.

**Tracks:**

- **Bond** (0–3, start 1). Standing with your merchant house.
- **Debt** (0–6, start 2). At 6 the house makes a major demand, then resets to 3.
- **Heat** (0–4, start 0). Attention from marks, rivals, and the watch.

#### Advancement

**Basic** (5 XP each):

- ☐ Take another Trader-Thief move.
- ☐ Take another Trader-Thief move.
- ☐ Take a move from another playbook.
- ☐ Take a move from another playbook.
- ☐ Increase your maximum held Angles by 1.
- ☐ Build a route: name a smuggling road that is always open to you between two places.
- ☐ Train a move you lean on: it upgrades 2 dice to proficiency (never more than its stat).
- ☐ Buy down your Debt by 1, permanently, with a clever score.

**Advanced:**

- ☐ Raise one stat by 1 (max 5). Rare, and only at a true turning point.
- ☐ Go independent: cut ties with the house, resolving the fallout in play, and set up on your own.
- ☐ Found a trade-post: claim a Bastion facility (a market) for the Free Legion.
